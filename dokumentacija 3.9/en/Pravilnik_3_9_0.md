# Rulebook on the KOLO System

*Version 3.9.0*

*KOLO is a mutual support system based on the idea that people can record what they contribute to each other and to the community. This Rulebook governs how that system works: who its actors are, what the common good they collectively build consists of, how contributions are recorded, and how decisions are made. The Rulebook is a binding act whose provisions govern the rights and obligations of users.*

## Chapter I — General Provisions

### Article 1

This Rulebook governs the fundamental concepts, structure, and operating rules of the system, including:

— the common good as the purpose of the system — its concept, content, licensing and rules of contribution — and the actors and instruments of the system: the KOLO Foundation, the KOLO Protocol, and the KOLO Community;

— POEN and the contribution ledger;

— ZRNO, the accounting coefficient, and the accounting framework of the system;

— user statuses and proof of reality;

— contribution to the common good — founding, financial, operational contribution, and sponsorship;

— development phases of the system and system governance;

— system modules;

— transparency and protection of personal data;

— the legal nature and structural limits of the KOLO system;

— liability and risks of users, and dispute resolution;

— amendments to this Rulebook and the system of derived acts.

This Rulebook governs the listed areas at the framework level. Detailed parameters, tables, procedures, and operational mechanics are established by special rulebooks that reference this act.

Rules on access to the platform, use of the goods and services exchange space, and other matters of technical platform use are governed by the Terms of Use.

### Article 2

Individual terms in this Rulebook have the following meanings:

**KOLO system** — a participatory common-good system; the functional whole comprising the common good, the actors and instruments of the system, and users, organized for the purpose of recording contributions and participation in the common good. The KOLO system is not a legal entity nor a special legal category.

**Common good** — the collective good of all users; the purpose of the KOLO system. It comprises the system software, system rules, the contribution and participation ledger, and content created in the system.

**KOLO Foundation (Foundation)** — a non-profit private legal entity established under the Law on Endowments and Foundations, headquartered in Sombor; the legal instrument of the system and guardian of the common good.

**KOLO Protocol (Protocol)** — the technical mechanism of the common good that maintains the ledger, calculates the accounting coefficient, and applies the system rules; it has no legal personality.

**KOLO Community (Community)** — a descriptive term for the totality of system users; it is not a legal entity and does not make decisions.

**Core system** — the minimal set of elements of the KOLO system that functions from the outset; comprises the common good, the Protocol, the Foundation, the Community, users, POEN, ZRNO, the accounting coefficient, proof of reality, and the contribution recording channels.

**Module** — an extension that adds functionality to the core system without changing it; activated according to its own prerequisites.

**POEN** — the internal accounting unit of the system; a record in the Protocol's ledger by which contributions and other forms of participation in the common good are recorded.

**ZRNO** — the internal accounting unit of the system by which a user's position in the common good is recorded in the Protocol's ledger.

**Accounting coefficient** — the numerical ratio of the total number of recorded POENs to the number of ZRNOs available for inscription in the Protocol.

**Accounting period** — the time interval at the end of which the Protocol calculates the accounting coefficient and applies the rules for ZRNO inscription and write-off, and changes in ZRNO status.

**Zero-sum invariant** — the property of the system whereby the sum of all POEN records, including the Protocol's record, equals zero at all times.

**Proof of reality** — a model of user verification based on personal acquaintance, which confirms the reality, uniqueness, and continuity of a user.

**Vouching chain** — the mechanism of proof of reality in which verified users confirm the reality of new users on the basis of direct personal acquaintance.

**Reality index** — a numerical value (0–100%) expressing the degree of a user's confirmation and determining the scope of access to system functions and verification capacity.

**Unverified user** — a person registered on the platform whose reality has not been confirmed through the vouching chain.

**Verified user** — a person whose reality has been confirmed through the vouching chain in accordance with the proof-of-reality rules.

**ZRNO Holder** — a verified user to whom ZRNO has been inscribed in the Protocol.

**Pseudonym** — the username under which the user is visible in the system and in the public ledger.

**Founding contribution** — work performed before the platform's launch whose contribution is retroactively recorded in POENs; one-time and time-limited.

**Financial contribution** — a dinar inflow to the Foundation on the basis of donations from natural persons and sponsorship by legal entities and entrepreneurs.

**Operational contribution** — an activity outside the platform whose contribution is recorded in POENs after verification of completion.

**Sponsor** — a legal entity or entrepreneur that supports the Foundation's operations by donating money, goods, or services; it is not a system user and does not receive POEN or ZRNO.

**Automatic recording** — the recording of POENs as an automatic act of the Protocol executed pursuant to system rules, without the discretionary decision of any actor.

**Circle** — an organizational unit of the system based on shared interest or activity; it has no legal personality.

**Cooperative** — a local organizational unit of the system based on the territorial principle, registered under the Law on Cooperatives; it has full legal personality.

**Collective forms** — the collective term for organizational units of the system that connect users into more permanent structures: Circles and Cooperatives.

**Gornje Kolo (Upper Kolo)** — the governing body of the system composed of all ZRNO Holders; it makes decisions on system rules.

**Management Board (UO)** — the body of the KOLO Foundation whose composition, mandate, and competencies are governed by the Statute.

**Protective veto** — the right of the Foundation to refuse the execution of an Upper Kolo decision that would jeopardize the operational and financial sustainability of the Foundation before it attains financial self-sufficiency.

**Quadratic voting** — the decision-making mechanism in the Upper Kolo in which voting power equals the integer value of the square root of the number of active ZRNOs.

**Whitepaper** — the conceptual and public document describing the KOLO system.

Terms not defined in this Article have the meaning established in the relevant chapters of this Rulebook or in special rulebooks.

### Article 3

This Rulebook is the overarching operational act of the KOLO system. Special rulebooks and other derived acts must be consistent with this Rulebook.

This Rulebook is adopted and amended by the Management Board of the KOLO Foundation, following the procedure specifically governed by this Rulebook.

The language of the system is Serbian.

## Chapter II — The Common Good, Actors, and Instruments

### Article 4

The KOLO system consists of three elements: the common good as that which users share and to which they contribute; the Foundation as the legal instrument that ensures the conditions for the functioning of the system; and the Protocol as the software that maintains the ledger and applies the rules. Users form the Community, but the Community is not a body nor a legal entity — users' participation in system governance is exercised through the Upper Kolo, in accordance with this Rulebook.

This chapter governs each of those elements: what the common good is and under what conditions it is used, what the Foundation does and what it may not do, what the Protocol can do and of what it is deprived.

### Article 5

The common good is the purpose of the KOLO system. It comprises the system software, system rules, the contribution and participation ledger, and content created in the system.

The common good is the collective good of all users. No user, nor founder, nor the Foundation has ownership rights over the common good or the right to dispose of it.

The common good does not constitute collective ownership within the meaning of applicable property law categories. The user's relationship to the common good is participatory — a right to use and to contribute, not a right to dispose.

The collective character of the common good is ensured by the common-good licenses, the legal structure of the Foundation as its guardian, and the four principles of the system.

### Article 6

The infrastructure on which the system operates — servers, databases, network equipment, and other technical resources — is not a constituent part of the common good. The ledger as a record and the content belong to the common good; the database as a storage medium belongs to the infrastructure.

Infrastructure is the operational prerequisite for the functioning of the system. Its provision and maintenance is a service obligation of the Foundation.

The Foundation has the right and duty to select, change, and upgrade the infrastructure. No such change affects the status or integrity of the common good.

### Article 7

The system software is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License, version 3.0 (AGPL-3.0).

Content created in the system is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0).

The moral rights of authors of software and content remain intact and are not transferred.

The software and content of the common good may not be re-licensed under more restrictive terms; only replacement with a license of equivalent copyleft, or share-alike regime, is permitted.

The system brand — the name "KOLO", the logotype, the mark, and the domains — is not part of the common good and is under the exclusive control of the Foundation.

### Article 8

Contributions to the software are accepted under the terms of the Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO), by which the contributor confirms the right to contribute the code under the common-good license.

Contributions to content are accepted upon acceptance of the common-good license.

Contributions belong to the common good.

### Article 9

The KOLO Foundation is the legal instrument of the system and the guardian of the common good. The Foundation is not the owner of the system or of the common good.

The Foundation provides and maintains the infrastructure, receives dinar donations, finances the costs and programs of the system, and represents the system in legal transactions.

The Foundation does not acquire POEN or ZRNO and does not participate in the accounting framework of the system. The Foundation's relationship with the system is exclusively in dinar funds.

The internal organization, bodies, mandates, and decision-making procedures of the Foundation are governed by the Statute.

### Article 10

The KOLO Protocol is the technical mechanism of the common good. The Protocol maintains the ledger, calculates the accounting coefficient, and applies the system rules.

All Protocol operations are deterministic and algorithmic, without discretion. The Protocol cannot act outside the system rules.

The Protocol has no legal personality. The Protocol does not enter into contracts, does not hold accounts, does not enter into legal relationships with users, and is not a controller of personal data.

The rules pursuant to which the Protocol operates are established in accordance with this Rulebook.

### Article 11

The KOLO Community comprises all system users as collective guardians of the common good.

The Community is not a legal entity, has no bodies or representatives, and as a whole does not make decisions.

Users' participation in system governance is exercised through the Upper Kolo, in accordance with this Rulebook.

## Chapter III — POEN and the Contribution Ledger

### Article 12

POEN is the way the system records who has contributed how much and who has received how much. Every contribution, every donation, every exchange of goods or services between users — all of it is recorded in POENs. POEN is not money and cannot be converted into money; it is an internal record in the system.

This chapter governs how POEN is created, how the ledger functions, what it means for the system to be in equilibrium (zero-sum), and through which channels contributions are recorded.

### Article 13

POEN is the internal accounting unit of the system by which contributions and other forms of participation in the common good are recorded. POEN serves to let users know how much they have contributed and how much they have exchanged within the system; the closest analogy is a record in a registry — it records a fact, but is not in itself a means that can be spent outside the system.

New POEN records are inscribed exclusively by the Protocol through the contribution recording channels.

POEN has no bearer; it exists solely as a record in the Protocol's ledger. POEN amounts are expressed in whole numbers. POEN does not represent money, currency, electronic money, a means of payment, digital assets, a financial instrument, or a security. POEN cannot be converted into a means with value outside the system.

### Article 14

The sum of all POEN records in the system, including the Protocol's record, equals zero at all times. This property ensures that POENs cannot be created without a counter-entry — every recorded contribution has an exact counter-entry in the Protocol's ledger.

The Protocol maintains a record that is negative at all times and represents the counter-entry for all POENs recorded with users and organizational units.

No user or collective form may have a negative POEN record.

The exchange of goods and services redistributes existing POENs and does not change their total number in the system. The total number of POENs changes exclusively through the inscription of new records via the contribution recording channels.

### Article 15

The Protocol inscribes new POEN records through the following contribution recording channels:

1) operational contribution;

2) verification of other users in the vouching chain;

3) financial contribution;

4) sponsorship;

5) growth of collective forms;

6) social programs;

7) founding contribution.

The channels under items 1, 3, 4, and 7 are governed by Chapter VI of this Rulebook. The channel under item 2 is governed by the Rulebook on Proof of Reality. The channels under items 5 and 6 are governed in Chapter VIII as system modules. The recording parameters, conditions, and limitations for each channel are established by special rulebooks, in particular the Rulebook on Operational Contribution, the Rulebook on Proof of Reality, the Rulebook on Donations, the Rulebook on Sponsorship, the Rulebook on Collective Forms, the Support Program rulebooks, and the Rulebook on Founding Contribution.

Financial contribution is made in dinars. Sponsorship is made by donating money, goods, or services. Dinar funds enter the Foundation and do not enter the accounting framework of the system. Upon receipt of a donation or a sponsorship contribution, the Protocol records the contribution in POENs in the record of the relevant user, pursuant to the recording parameters established by a special rulebook. The recording of POENs is an automatic act of the Protocol and does not create a contractual relationship between the donor and the Foundation.

### Article 16

Users within the system exchange goods and services. The Protocol records each exchange by updating the POEN ledger: the record of the user providing the good or service is increased, and the record of the receiving user is decreased by the same amount.

Exchange redistributes existing POENs among users and does not increase their total number in the system. The Protocol updates the ledger when users conduct an exchange.

Exchange is not a payment transaction. The ledger update of POENs is not a transfer of monetary value and does not constitute a payment within the meaning of payment services regulations.

For performance, quality, liability, and risk in exchange, the users who participate in it are responsible, pursuant to the general rules of the law of obligations. The Foundation and the Protocol do not mediate in the exchange and are not responsible for the performance of users' obligations.

All users may exchange goods and services and initiate a POEN ledger update in favor of another user (with or without a counter-service). The preview of listings in the platform's advertising space — the content of an offer or request, price, location, and pseudonym of the advertiser — is public and available to all visitors for the accessibility of exchange. Posting listings, accessing the advertiser's contact details, and communicating regarding listings are available exclusively to verified users. For non-registered and unverified persons, the advertiser's pseudonym in the advertising space is not linked to the contribution ledger, account balance, or user profile. The rules of the platform's advertising space are governed by the Terms of Use.

## Chapter IV — ZRNO and the Accounting Framework

### Article 17

ZRNO is the second record the system maintains about the user. While POEN records contribution, ZRNO records position — the measure of a user's more permanent participation in the common good, on the basis of which the user acquires a vote in decisions about system rules. The accounting coefficient is the numerical ratio that connects POEN and ZRNO.

This chapter governs what ZRNO is, how it is inscribed and written off, what states it has, why it cannot be transferred, and how the accounting coefficient is calculated and what the accounting period is.

### Article 18

ZRNO is the internal accounting unit of the system by which a user's position in the common good is recorded in the Protocol's ledger. The position arises from the user's contribution and gives the user a vote in decisions about system rules. ZRNO does not represent a share, a stock, a security, a collective investment unit, an investment contract, or digital assets, and carries no dividend, interest, return, or right to liquidation proceeds.

The total number of ZRNOs is 1,000,000 and cannot be increased or decreased.

At any given time, one part of the ZRNOs is inscribed with users and another part is available for inscription in the Protocol; the sum of inscribed and available ZRNOs always equals the total number.

ZRNO is inscribed and written off exclusively by the Protocol, at the user's request and pursuant to the system rules. The governance function — the right to vote in the Upper Kolo — arises from inscribed and activated ZRNO; ZRNO is not in itself a voting right, but a record of position from which that right arises.

### Article 19

A verified user inscribes ZRNO when they satisfy the conditions established by the system rules. By inscribing ZRNO, the user becomes a ZRNO Holder.

Inscription of ZRNO requires a minimum of 20,000 recorded POENs in the user's record. In one accounting period, a user may use at most 1% of their recorded POENs for ZRNO inscription.

An exception to the conditions of this Article at the start of the system is governed by the transitional provision (Article 82).

ZRNO inscription is initiated by the user. The Protocol verifies the fulfilment of conditions and executes the inscription if conditions are met. Inscription is not a discretionary decision and no system actor approves or rejects it.

Upon ZRNO inscription, the Protocol reduces the user's recorded POENs pursuant to the accounting coefficient applicable for the current accounting period.

### Article 20

Inscribed ZRNO has two states: free and active.

Free ZRNO is inscribed ZRNO without a governance function. The holder of free ZRNO may activate it or write it off.

Active ZRNO is ZRNO that the holder has activated for participation in governance; active ZRNO grants the right to vote in the Upper Kolo. Active ZRNO cannot be written off until the holder returns it to the free state.

Transitions between ZRNO states and their effects occur at the end of the accounting period.

### Article 21

A ZRNO Holder may write off free ZRNO — return it to the Protocol.

Upon write-off, the Protocol records POENs to the holder pursuant to the accounting coefficient applicable for the current accounting period.

Active ZRNO cannot be written off. A holder who wishes to write off active ZRNO must first return it to the free state.

A user whose ZRNO has been entirely written off regains the status of a verified user.

The difference between the accounting coefficient at the time of inscription and at the time of write-off is not a return. It is an arithmetic consequence of a change in the ledger state of the whole system; no actor pays it or guarantees it. POENs recorded upon write-off have the same status as all other POENs.

### Article 22

ZRNO cannot be transferred to another user.

ZRNO is bound to the user whose reality has been confirmed through the vouching chain.

There is no market for ZRNO, there is no price for ZRNO, and there is no possibility of trading it.

### Article 23

The accounting coefficient is the numerical ratio of the total number of recorded POENs to the number of ZRNOs available for inscription in the Protocol. The accounting coefficient indicates how many POENs are needed to inscribe one ZRNO in a given accounting period.

The accounting coefficient is calculated by the Protocol, automatically and without discretion.

The accounting coefficient changes with activity in the system: the inscription of new POEN records and the inscription and write-off of ZRNOs shift the coefficient. The exchange of goods and services between users does not change the coefficient.

The accounting coefficient is an administrative variable. It is not a price, not an exchange rate, and not a performance index; it does not express the value of ZRNO or POEN outside the system.

### Article 24

The accounting period is the time interval at the end of which the Protocol calculates the accounting coefficient.

At the end of the accounting period, the rules for ZRNO inscription and write-off are applied and transitions between ZRNO states occur.

The accounting period lasts twenty-four hours, from midnight to midnight.

### Article 25

A ZRNO Holder holds a position in the accounting system that shifts with the accounting coefficient, as well as the right to participate in system governance through the Upper Kolo.

A change in a ZRNO Holder's position arises exclusively as a consequence of activity in the system and a change in the accounting coefficient. That change is not a return, is not guaranteed, and no actor pays it.

The benefit of the position is realized by the ZRNO Holder exclusively within the system. ZRNO and POEN have no value outside the system and cannot be converted into money.

A ZRNO Holder who does not actively participate in the system retains their recorded position; any change in that position is a consequence of the activity of other users in the system, not an action of the system directed at the individual.

## Chapter V — User Statuses, Proof of Reality, and Termination of Status

### Article 26

For a user to be able to exchange, to have contributions recorded, or to govern the system, the system must know that the person is a real and unique individual. This chapter governs the statuses a user may hold, the way in which a user's status as a real person is confirmed, and what happens when a user ceases to be part of the system.

The operational elements of proof of reality and the mechanics of status termination are governed by a special rulebook.

### Article 27

A user in the KOLO system may hold one of three statuses: unverified user, verified user, and ZRNO Holder.

A user transitions from one status to another when they satisfy the conditions established by the system rules.

Status determines the scope of a user's rights and capabilities in the system.

### Article 28

An unverified user is a person registered on the platform whose reality has not been confirmed through the vouching chain.

An unverified user has the right to view public system content, including viewing listings in the platform's advertising space. They may exchange goods and services outside the platform's advertising space and participate in POEN ledger updates — as a giver or a receiver (with or without a counter-service). An unverified user may not post listings, access advertisers' contact details, or communicate via the Platform, except by presenting themselves on the guarantee board. An unverified user may not have contributions recorded in POENs, nor inscribe ZRNO.

### Article 29

A verified user is a person whose reality has been confirmed through the vouching chain in accordance with the proof-of-reality rules.

A verified user may exchange goods and services, have contributions recorded in POENs, participate in collective forms and social programs, and confirm the reality of other users in the vouching chain.

A verified user inscribes ZRNO when they satisfy the inscription conditions, thereby becoming a ZRNO Holder.

### Article 30

A ZRNO Holder is a verified user to whom ZRNO has been inscribed in the Protocol. The first ZRNO Holders at the start of the system are established in the manner set out in the transitional provision of this Rulebook (Article 82).

A ZRNO Holder has all the rights of a verified user. In addition, by activating ZRNO they participate in system governance through the Upper Kolo and hold a position in the accounting system, as well as a permanent capacity to confirm the reality of other users.

A ZRNO Holder whose ZRNO has been entirely written off regains the status of a verified user.

### Article 31

Proof of reality is a model of user verification based on personal acquaintance. It confirms that the user is a real person, that they are unique — one person, one user — and that they are continuously the same person.

Proof of reality does not require the collection of personal documents or the unique citizen identification number. No centralized register exists in the system linking the user's pseudonym to their identity.

Verification of reality is the processing of personal data. The legal basis for that processing is the execution of the contractual relationship between the user and the Foundation.

### Article 32

A user's reality is confirmed through the vouching chain: verified users confirm the reality of new users on the basis of direct personal acquaintance. Every new user enters the system because someone who is already verified knows them and confirms that they exist.

Every user has a reality index — a numerical value expressing the degree of confirmation of the user. The reality index determines the scope of access to system functions and the user's capacity to confirm the reality of others.

The set of confirmations in the vouching chain constitutes the verification graph. The verification graph is a record of personal data; it is maintained on the infrastructure on which the Protocol operates, with data minimization.

The Platform may provide a mechanism through which an unverified user may present themselves to the network of existing verified users for the purpose of establishing contact necessary for the conduct of verification. The existence of that mechanism does not change the nature, procedure, or conditions of verification established by this Rulebook and the Rulebook on Proof of Reality. The rules for publication, content, and data processing within that mechanism are established by the Terms of Use and the Privacy Policy.

The rules for calculating the reality index, the rules for expanding the vouching network, and the measures against abuse of the vouching chain are established by a special rulebook.

### Article 33

A user's status terminates by withdrawal, exclusion, or death.

Withdrawal is the voluntary termination of status, on the basis of the user's electronic declaration, without a notice period.

Exclusion is the termination of status by a Foundation decision, due to a serious breach of system rules.

A user's access to system functions may be temporarily restricted by suspension; during suspension the user retains their status but may not use the functions to which the suspension applies.

The grounds, procedure, and mechanics of each mode of status termination and suspension are governed by the Terms of Use.

### Article 34

Upon termination of status, the user's active ZRNO transitions to the free state, after which all free ZRNO is written off and returned to the available ZRNOs in the Protocol. The write-off of ZRNO upon status termination does not trigger the recording of POENs pursuant to the accounting coefficient.

The user's POEN records are annulled, and the Protocol's corresponding counter-entry is reduced by the same amount. The zero-sum invariant remains preserved.

The annulment of POEN records and the return of ZRNO to the available pool changes the accounting coefficient on both levels — the numerator (total recorded POENs) decreases and the denominator (available ZRNOs) increases.

No monetary compensation is paid to the user.

Upon status termination, the anonymization of user data is initiated: the email address and voluntarily submitted data are deleted, the user's connections in the verification graph are anonymized, and the numerical history is retained under an identifier that no longer enables identification. Records thus anonymized cease to be personal data.

POEN and ZRNO records are not inherited as property rights. The handling of ledger records in the event of a user's death is governed in more detail by the Terms of Use.

## Chapter VI — Contribution to the Common Good

### Article 35

The common good does not arise by itself — it arises through contribution. In addition to the exchange of goods and services between users, which redistributes existing POENs, the system records new POENs through the channels established in Article 15 of this Rulebook. This chapter elaborates four of those channels — operational contribution, donations, sponsorship, and founding contribution. The verification of other users as a form of contribution is governed by the Rulebook on Proof of Reality. The channels for growth of collective forms and social programs are governed in Chapter VIII as system modules.

Recording parameters, procedures, and limitations are established by special rulebooks.

### Article 36

Operational contribution is an activity outside the platform whose contribution is recorded in POENs after verification of completion.

The Foundation, the Upper Kolo, or ZRNO Holders publish a task to be performed for the common good. The user voluntarily applies for the execution of the task. After completion, ZRNO Holders verify the completion before recording the contribution. In Phase 1, while there are no ZRNO Holders in the system, the verification function is performed by members of the Foundation's Management Board. All applications are publicly visible to all system users.

Operational contribution may encompass a wide spectrum of activities — from organizing a local event, through technical work on infrastructure, to promoting the system in the community.

Operational contribution does not establish an employment relationship within the meaning of Article 5 of the Labour Law — there is no subordination, personal obligation to work, or remuneration.

The procedure for applying for, executing, and verifying operational contribution is established by a special rulebook.

### Article 37

Founding contribution is the work performed before the platform's launch — system design, protocol development, and legal and organizational preparation — whose contribution is retroactively recorded in POENs.

Founding contribution is in its nature an operational contribution performed before the system existed. The Protocol records that contribution retroactively through a special channel.

The recording of founding contribution is gradual, in steps of a fixed amount, tied to the cumulative growth of the total number of POENs.

Founding contribution is limited. When the Protocol has recorded the full amount up to a pre-determined upper limit, the channel closes permanently.

POENs recorded through this channel are entered in the records of the founders — the natural persons who performed the work before the platform's launch — and have the same status as all other POENs. Founding contribution is a separate recording channel and does not affect the operational contribution limit.

The upper limit of founding contribution, the amount and schedule of recording steps, and the point at which the channel closes are established by a special rulebook.

### Article 38

Financial contribution is a dinar inflow to the Foundation on the basis of donations by natural persons and sponsorship by legal entities and entrepreneurs.

Both forms rest on the same principle: a non-refundable donation to the Foundation whose contribution is recorded in POENs as an automatic act of the Protocol. Dinar funds enter the Foundation and do not enter the accounting framework of the system.

When dinar inflows exceed the Foundation's operational costs, the surplus is directed to system programs. The rules for distributing the surplus are determined by the founder and the Foundation in Phase 1, and by the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

### Article 39

A natural person's donation is a dinar inflow to the Foundation on the basis of a donation agreement.

Upon receipt of a donation, the Protocol records the contribution in the donor's record in POENs, pursuant to the recording parameters established by a special rulebook. The recording of POENs is an automatic act of the Protocol and does not create a contractual relationship between the donor and the Foundation with respect to POENs.

The Foundation issues a donation receipt upon the donor's request, in accordance with the law. The tax treatment of the donation depends on the donor's status, the Foundation's registered status, and the applicable tax regulations at the time of the donation.

The recording parameters for donations, including the donation recording coefficient and donation levels, are established by a special rulebook.

### Article 40

A sponsor is a legal entity or entrepreneur that supports the Foundation's operations by donating money, goods, or services. A sponsor is not a system user and does not receive POEN or ZRNO.

The sponsorship contribution is recorded in the record of the natural person who is a verified system user — the owner of the legal entity, or the entrepreneur themselves.

The relationship between the Foundation and the sponsor is governed by a donation agreement. Every donation is recorded at the time of receipt. The Foundation maintains a record of the link between the sponsor and the user in whose record the contribution is entered. The procedure for identifying the beneficial owner and the rules in cases of multiple or indirect ownership are established by a special rulebook.

## Chapter VII — System Governance

### Article 41

Every system has rules, and someone must set, change, and ensure their application.

The KOLO system resolves this through progressive decentralization — a structured pathway from centralized to decentralized governance with measurable transition conditions. Governance begins with the founder and the Foundation and is progressively transferred to the Community through the Upper Kolo.

This chapter governs the two governance phases, the conditions for transition from one to the other, the composition and decision-making procedure of the Upper Kolo, the vote delegation mechanism, the Foundation's protective veto, and the limits of governance power. Voting procedures and the operational rules of the Upper Kolo are established by the Rulebook on the Upper Kolo.

### Article 42

System governance proceeds in two phases.

In Phase 1, the founder establishes the Protocol's rules in cooperation with the Foundation. Phase 1 lasts until the total number of POENs recorded in the system reaches 1,000,000.

In Phase 2, the Upper Kolo becomes the governing body of the system. The Foundation retains its service and executive role.

The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 occurs automatically upon reaching the threshold and does not require a special decision.

### Article 43

In Phase 1, the founder has discretion to change the system's rules and adjust parameters based on initial experience.

The founder's discretion is not unlimited. The founder may not amend the four principles, may not change the licenses under which the common good has been published, and may not appropriate the common good.

The limitations on the founder's discretion are embedded in this Rulebook as a normative act of the Foundation, and simultaneously in the technical architecture of the system.

### Article 44

The threshold for transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 is 1,000,000 recorded POENs in the system.

Reaching the threshold simultaneously activates the possibility of ZRNO inscription and establishes the Upper Kolo.

In the accounting logic, the threshold of one million recorded POENs corresponds to a Protocol record state of −1,000,000.

### Article 45

The Upper Kolo is the governing body of the system, composed of all ZRNO Holders.

The Upper Kolo is formed automatically upon the activation of ZRNO — as soon as the first users inscribe ZRNO under the system's rules, they constitute the Upper Kolo.

The Upper Kolo decides on Protocol rules, on the activation and deactivation of modules, and on all matters affecting the common good, except matters excluded from its competence in accordance with this Rulebook.

The Upper Kolo is not a legal entity.

### Article 46

The Upper Kolo makes decisions by quadratic voting — a mechanism in which voting power equals the integer value of the square root of the user's active ZRNOs, rounded down.

Free ZRNO does not confer voting power. A holder who wishes to vote must activate ZRNO, thereby relinquishing the possibility of write-off until they return it to the free state.

ZRNO is not spent by voting. Voting power arises from recorded ZRNO, not from the number of POENs, and not from dinar donations.

The types of decisions, thresholds for decision-making, quorum, and voting procedure are established by the Rulebook on the Upper Kolo.

### Article 47

ZRNO Holders who do not wish or are unable to participate in voting may delegate their votes to another ZRNO Holder.

Delegation is general — the delegate votes on behalf of the delegator on all matters while the delegation lasts.

Delegated votes are added to the delegate's own votes.

The rules of delegation, including the effects of revocation and delegation limitations, are established by the Rulebook on the Upper Kolo.

### Article 48

The Foundation has the right to refuse the execution of an Upper Kolo decision that would jeopardize the operational and financial sustainability of the Foundation before it attains financial self-sufficiency — in particular decisions on the expenditure of dinar funds (including collective procurements) that would impair the Foundation's ability to cover basic costs and maintain the system's infrastructure.

The protective veto is not discretionary. The Foundation must justify every veto by reference to a specific threat to the Foundation's sustainability. A veto without justification is an abuse subject to liability under this Rulebook.

### Article 49

The protective veto is extinguished permanently and irreversibly when the Foundation's financial resources reach the threshold of financial self-sufficiency established by a special rulebook.

The extinguishment of the veto is irreversible.

The extinguishment of the veto does not abolish the statutory obligations of the Management Board.

### Article 50

The Upper Kolo does not have unlimited power. Three limitations are embedded in the system.

The first limitation consists of the four principles. No Upper Kolo decision may abolish non-convertibility, introduce property rights over records, make donations refundable, or abandon the principle of data minimization.

The second limitation is the Foundation's protective veto while in force, and the statutory obligations of the Management Board after the veto is extinguished.

The third limitation consists of the common-good licenses. The Upper Kolo may not replace AGPL-3.0 and CC BY-SA 4.0 with more restrictive licenses.

### Article 51

In Phase 2, the Foundation retains its service role — it provides and maintains the infrastructure, represents the system in legal transactions, and implements Upper Kolo decisions. The Foundation's role is executive, not governance, with retention of the Management Board's statutory responsibilities.

With regard to the distribution of dinar funds, the Upper Kolo issues recommendations to the Foundation's Management Board. The Management Board considers recommendations and applies them within its statutory powers under the Law on Endowments and Foundations, with an obligation to give a reasoned response to each recommendation.

### Article 52

In the event of the Foundation's dissolution, the common good does not cease to exist. The software and content remain available under the license terms.

The ledger and infrastructure are transferred to a legal successor that accepts the system's principles and the obligations of a common-good guardian.

The rules of transfer are established by the Statute and a special Foundation act.

## Chapter VIII — System Modules

### Article 53

The KOLO system separates the core system from modules. The core constitutes the minimal set of elements without which the system does not exist. Modules are extensions that add functionality to the core and are activated according to their own prerequisites.

This chapter governs modules at the framework level — the operational rules of each module are established by special rulebooks.

Among modules, a distinction is drawn between collective forms — Circle and Cooperative — as organizational units connecting users into more permanent structures, and other modules that govern special participation regimes.

### Article 54

A module is an extension that adds functionality to the core system.

The order in which modules are activated depends on the Community's needs and is not predetermined.

New modules may be added by decision of the Foundation in Phase 1, and of the Upper Kolo in Phase 2, provided they do not undermine the system's principles.

### Article 55

A Circle is a collective form based on shared interest or activity.

Circles are formed by the association of users. Existing associations and cooperatives registered under applicable laws may transfer their structure into a Circle that mirrors their composition and organization.

Circles have an incentive function through the growth mechanism — the Protocol records new POENs in accordance with the number of Circle members and the reaching of defined thresholds. POENs arising from this mechanism are recorded in the Circle's record as an organizational unit.

A Circle has no legal personality. An association or cooperative that forms a Circle retains its legal personality independently of the Circle.

The rules for the formation, functioning, and dissolution of Circles are established by a special rulebook.

### Article 56

A Cooperative is a collective form based on the territorial principle, registered under the Law on Cooperatives, with full legal personality.

A Cooperative within the system has two functions. The first is local coordination — the structure through which exchange, communication, and the organization of activities takes place in the territory it covers. The second is the growth mechanism — the reaching of user number thresholds is recorded in POENs in the Cooperative's record, by the same mechanism as for Circles.

The relationship between the Foundation and the Cooperative is governed by a cooperation agreement, with the Cooperative retaining full autonomy as an independent legal entity. The Cooperative does not become the owner of any part of the common good.

A Cooperative, as a legal entity registered under the Law on Cooperatives, has its own legal obligations — maintaining business books, annual reporting, and compliance with cooperative principles. The rules of cooperation between the Foundation and cooperatives are established by a special rulebook.

### Article 57

Social programs are a mechanism for the automatic recording of POENs for groups of users whose structural participation in the common good the Protocol recognizes even though it is not expressed through individual activities.

The automatic recording of new POENs for qualifying groups has a redistributive effect: new records increase the total number of recorded POENs in the system, thereby changing the accounting coefficient for all users. This effect is a deliberate design decision — the system recognizes that participation that is by its nature continuous and diffuse cannot be recorded through individual activities.

The initial qualifying groups are mothers (primary caregivers), elderly users, users in a special care regime, and users in education. New groups may be added by decision of the Foundation in Phase 1, and of the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

A user who belongs to a qualifying group verifies the data that confirms that status. After verification, the Protocol automatically records new POEN entries for that user without the need for a specific activity.

Automatic recording in social programs is not social assistance or a benefit — it is automatic recording in POENs that enables users to participate in the system on a more equal footing.

Social programs require the verification of special categories of personal data within the meaning of the Law on Personal Data Protection. The legal basis for the processing of those data is the explicit consent of the user participating in the social program. Consent may be withdrawn at any time, with the consequence of ceasing the automatic recording of POENs.

The qualifying groups, the conditions for status verification, and the recording parameters are established by a special rulebook. The activation of this module requires an update of the data protection impact assessment.

### Article 58

This module defines a special regime of rights, limitations, and protections for underage users of the system, with additional restrictions for persons under fifteen years of age in accordance with Article 16 of the Law on Personal Data Protection.

A minor user may not access the system independently — access requires the consent of a parent or legal guardian.

A minor user has a limited scope of activities in the system. A minor user may not inscribe ZRNO or participate in governance through the Upper Kolo until the age of eighteen.

The processing of minors' data is subject to enhanced requirements. The consent of the parent or legal guardian is the legal prerequisite for processing.

The rules for access, the scope of permitted activities, exchange restrictions, and measures for the protection of underage users are established by a special rulebook. The activation of this module requires an update of the data protection impact assessment.

### Article 59

Internationalization is the infrastructural expansion of the system into new regions. The system expands its infrastructure, ledger, and rules to new territories, retaining a unified Protocol and a unified common-good ledger.

Expansion into new regions requires adaptations in several dimensions: the legal framework of the target jurisdiction, linguistic localization of the platform, establishment of a local vouching chain, and the possible formation of local cooperatives.

The prerequisite for activating this module is a stable system with an active Upper Kolo, sufficient experience with the system's operation in the primary region, and a legal analysis for the target jurisdictions. The decision to expand is made by the Upper Kolo.

Expansion into the territory of the European Union requires full compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation. Expansion into other jurisdictions requires analysis of local regulations on personal data protection, digital assets, foundations, and cooperatives.

## Chapter IX — Transparency and Protection of Personal Data

### Article 60

The KOLO system by its nature processes personal data — the verification graph, the contribution ledger, donation data, and, in the context of certain modules, special categories of data. At the same time, the system rests on the principle of data minimization as one of its four principles.

This chapter governs how the system approaches data protection: two design decisions that shape the protection architecture, the legal basis for processing and the Foundation's position as controller, the relationship between the right to erasure and the integrity of the ledger, the Foundation's obligations, and users' rights. The system applies the Law on Personal Data Protection.

The technical and organizational protection measures, the privacy policy, and the procedures for exercising users' rights are established by special acts.

### Article 61

Data protection in the system is based on data protection by design and by default.

The first design decision is pseudonymity of the ledger. Records in the ledger are tied to pseudonyms, not to personal names. No centralized table exists that links pseudonyms to personal identities of users. Pseudonymity is not anonymity — pseudonymized data remain personal data because they can, with additional information, be linked to an identified person.

The second design decision is data minimization. The Platform collects only the data necessary for the functioning of the system. A user may voluntarily enter additional data for easier use of the platform, but such entry is not a condition for proof of reality or for access to system functions.

### Article 62

The system processes users' personal data — platform user data, proof-of-reality data, voluntarily entered data, activity data, donation and sponsorship data, and, in the context of certain modules, special categories of data and data of minors.

The categories of data, their scope, purpose, and processing regime are established by the Privacy Policy and the Records of Processing Activities.

### Article 63

The KOLO Foundation is the data controller — it determines the purposes and means of processing. The Protocol is the technical means of processing. The processing of personal data is based on legal bases established by law; the legal basis for each category of data is established by the Privacy Policy and the Records of Processing Activities.

If the Foundation engages third parties for infrastructure maintenance, those parties are data processors. The relationship between the Foundation and processors is governed by a processing agreement in accordance with the law.

### Article 64

The user's right to erasure of personal data and the integrity of the common-good ledger are reconciled by the separation of identification and accounting data. The mechanics of anonymization upon status termination are governed by Article 34 of this Rulebook.

### Article 65

The Foundation is obliged to conduct a data protection impact assessment before commencing processing, to designate a data protection officer, and to apply technical and organizational protection measures proportionate to the risk.

The activation of modules that introduce the processing of special categories of data or data of minors requires an updated impact assessment before activation.

When the system infrastructure includes servers outside the Republic of Serbia, the transfer of personal data outside the country is subject to the rules on cross-border transfer in accordance with the law.

### Article 66

System users have all the rights guaranteed to them by law in connection with the protection of personal data. The Foundation provides an accessible mechanism for submitting requests and acts within the timeframes established by law.

The procedures for exercising users' rights are established by the Privacy Policy of the KOLO system.

### Article 67

The contribution ledger is public in pseudonymous form. Every user may verify records in the ledger and the consistency of the accounting framework.

The transparency of the ledger ensures user oversight of the system and reduces the possibility of manipulation.

The transparency of the ledger does not encompass data whose publication would infringe users' privacy — the verification graph, voluntarily entered data, and special categories of data. The pseudonymous contribution ledger is accessible to verified users, while visitors and unverified users see general indicators (aggregates) in accordance with Article 28. The viewing of listings in the platform's advertising space is governed separately (Art. 16) and is distinct from the contribution ledger. The scope of public data availability is established by the Privacy Policy.

## Chapter X — The Legal Nature of the KOLO System and the Four Principles

### Article 68

The KOLO system is not conceived or designed as a financial service, a payment service, or an investment scheme. This chapter governs what KOLO is and what it is not, and establishes the four principles that protect the legal position of the system.

The principles ensure that the system's accounting units cannot become money or a financial instrument, that the contribution ledger cannot become property that is traded, that donations cannot become the purchase of rights, and that the system does not collect data it does not need.

These principles are not rules that the system can change itself — they are the limits within which the system exists. Their amendment would change the legal nature of the system.

### Article 69

The KOLO system is a participatory common-good system based on the principles of social and solidarity economy and mutuality. The KOLO system is not a legal entity nor a special legal category.

The KOLO system is not a payment institution, an issuer of digital assets, a collective investment scheme, or an employer. The legal characterization of the system arises from its structural properties established by this Rulebook, in particular from the four principles and the rules on POEN, ZRNO, and operational contribution.

The relationship between users and the Foundation is a contractual relationship governed by the terms of use. The recording of POENs is not a counter-service for any obligation of the user towards the Foundation.

### Article 70

The legal position of the KOLO system rests on four principles: non-convertibility, the absence of property rights over records, the non-refundability of donations and automatic recording, and data minimization.

The four principles are structural limits of the system. They cannot be amended or abolished by any governance decision, including a decision of the Upper Kolo.

An amendment to this Rulebook that would amend or abolish any of the four principles is not permitted. A violation of any of the four principles changes the legal nature of the system.

The four principles are elaborated in the provisions that follow in this chapter.

### Article 71

No accounting unit of the KOLO system may be converted into money, currency, or any means with value outside the system, directly or indirectly.

The prohibition on conversion includes indirect conversion, including the exchange of an accounting unit for vouchers, gift cards, or other means with external value.

The Foundation does not redeem POENs or ZRNOs and does not provide for their conversion into dinars or any other means of payment.

### Article 72

Users do not have property rights over POEN and ZRNO records in the system's ledger. POEN and ZRNO records are data in the common-good ledger, not assets in the user's property.

POEN and ZRNO records are not transferred or inherited as property rights.

A recorded contribution and a recorded user position do not constitute a claim against the Foundation or a basis for a property claim.

### Article 73

A donation of money, goods, or services to the Foundation is non-refundable. The donor does not acquire, on the basis of the donation, a right to a refund, a governance right in the Foundation, or a stake in the system.

The recording of POENs is an automatic act of the Protocol executed pursuant to system rules. The recording of POENs is not a counter-service for a donation or for any other obligation of the user towards the Foundation.

There is no exchange agreement of dinars for POENs. The donation agreement does not contain provisions on POEN or on its recording.

The donor has no contractual or other claim against the Foundation for the recording of POENs. The recording of POENs is executed exclusively pursuant to system rules, independently of the act of donation.

### Article 74

Only data necessary for the functioning of the system are collected and processed in the system.

The Foundation does not store personal data of platform users in its own databases; such data are stored on the infrastructure on which the Protocol operates.

Data voluntarily submitted by a user for easier use of the platform may be amended or deleted by the user; their entry is not a condition for proof of reality or for access to system functions.

## Chapter XI — Liability, User Risks, and Dispute Resolution

### Article 75

Every system user assumes certain risks and bears certain responsibility. This chapter governs who is responsible for what in the system, what risks the user should understand before participating, and how disputes are resolved.

### Article 76

The Foundation is responsible for providing and maintaining the infrastructure, for the protection of personal data in accordance with the law, and for the application of system rules as established by the Rulebook.

The Foundation is not responsible for the performance of users' obligations in the exchange of goods and services. The Foundation does not mediate in exchange and is not a party to the legal relationship between users who exchange goods and services.

The Foundation does not guarantee that the system will function without interruption, that the infrastructure will not be affected by technical problems, or that the ledger will be protected from every security incident. The Foundation is obliged to take reasonable technical and organizational protection measures.

### Article 77

For performance, quality, and risk in the exchange of goods and services, the users who participate in it are responsible, pursuant to the general rules of the law of obligations.

The user is responsible for the accuracy of the data they submit to the system. A user who participates in the vouching chain is responsible for the truthfulness of the reality confirmation in accordance with the proof-of-reality rules.

A user who abuses the system is subject to measures in accordance with the rules on status termination and suspension.

### Article 78

Users should understand the following risks before participating in the system:

POEN and ZRNO have no value outside the system and cannot be converted into money. A recorded contribution does not constitute a claim against the Foundation.

The accounting coefficient changes with activity in the system. A change in a ZRNO Holder's position is not a return and is not guaranteed.

The Foundation may cease to exist. In that case, the role of common-good guardian and responsibility for infrastructure transfer to a legal successor in accordance with the Statute, but continuity of the system's functioning is not guaranteed.

The system may be affected by technical problems, security incidents, or regulatory changes that affect its functioning.

Donations to the Foundation are non-refundable, regardless of the further development of the system.

The exchange of goods and services within the system may have tax implications for the user. The Foundation does not provide tax advice. The user is responsible for their own tax obligations.

### Article 79

Disputes between users in connection with exchanges are resolved pursuant to the general rules of the law of obligations, before the competent court.

Disputes between users and the Foundation in connection with the application of system rules are resolved by agreement where possible. If agreement is not possible, the dispute is resolved before the competent court in Sombor, unless another local jurisdiction is determined by law.

Disputes in connection with the protection of personal data are resolved in accordance with the law, including the user's right to lodge a complaint with the Commissioner for Information of Public Importance and Personal Data Protection.

Internal dispute resolution mechanisms may be established by a special rulebook or a decision of the Upper Kolo.

## Chapter XII — Final Provisions

### Article 80

This Rulebook is adopted and amended by the Management Board of the KOLO Foundation.

In Phase 1, the Management Board amends the Rulebook on the proposal of the founder or on its own initiative. In Phase 2, the Management Board amends the Rulebook on the basis of an Upper Kolo decision, in accordance with the governance rules established by this Rulebook.

An amendment to this Rulebook that would amend or abolish any of the four principles is not permitted.

Every amendment to the Rulebook is published to system users before it takes effect within a period that allows users to familiarize themselves with the amendments. The minimum publication period before taking effect is established by a special rulebook.

### Article 81

This Rulebook is the overarching operational act. Special rulebooks elaborate the individual areas to which this Rulebook refers and must be consistent with this Rulebook.

Special rulebooks are adopted and amended following the same procedure as this Rulebook, unless otherwise established by this Rulebook.

The Terms of Use govern the rules of platform access, the use of the exchange space, and other matters of technical use, and may not conflict with this Rulebook.

The Privacy Policy governs the procedures for exercising users' rights in connection with personal data protection and must be consistent with this Rulebook and with the law.

The hierarchy of acts, their mutual ranking, and the rules for resolving inconsistencies are governed by the Rulebook on the Hierarchy of Acts.

### Article 82 — Start of the system (transitional provision)

When the system is put into operation, there are no previously verified users who could confirm the reality of the first users, nor recorded POENs from which ZRNO could be inscribed. For this reason, the first ZRNO Holders — the initial users, members of the Management Board of the Foundation — are established directly, by a decision of the Management Board of the Foundation.

The initial users are exempt, as a one-time event of the start of the system, from the conditions for ZRNO inscription under Article 19 (the minimum of recorded POENs) and from the rules of the vouching chain under Article 32 and the Rulebook on Proof of Reality that presuppose the existence of a prior verifier. They constitute the origin of the vouching chain; their initial reality index, exemption from supervision, and exemption from the anti-circular rule are governed by the Rulebook on Proof of Reality (Chapter VI — Initial Mechanism).

The initial users are not a separate status within the meaning of Article 27: they are ZRNO Holders with the attributes established by the Rulebook on Proof of Reality, and may at the same time exercise the operational powers of the Management Board in Phase 1. Once the system is established, all new ZRNO Holders acquire their status by the ordinary route (Art. 19, 29, and 30).

The number of initial users, the distribution of their founding contribution, and their operational powers are established by the Rulebook on Founding Contribution and the Statute of the Foundation.

### Article 83

This Rulebook enters into force on the day of its adoption by the Management Board of the KOLO Foundation.

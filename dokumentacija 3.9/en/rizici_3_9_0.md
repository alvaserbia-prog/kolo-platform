# Risk Acceptance Statement

*Version 3.9.0*

### Article 1 — Subject matter and legal nature

This Risk Acceptance Statement (hereinafter: the Statement) is a platform act of the KOLO system within the meaning of Article 10 of the Rulebook on the Hierarchy of Acts of the KOLO System. It describes the risks of participation in the KOLO system that the user should understand before joining the system.

The Statement applies together with the Terms of Use. In the event of any inconsistency between this Statement and the KOLO System Rulebook or any other rulebook, the solution more favourable to the user shall apply.

By joining the KOLO system, the user confirms that they have read, understood, and accepted the content of this Statement.

### Article 2 — Nature of the KOLO system

The KOLO system is a participatory common-good system based on the principles of social and solidarity economy and mutuality.

The KOLO system is not a financial service, payment service, investment scheme, collective investment scheme, payment institution, or issuer of digital assets. Participation in the KOLO system is not investing. Joining the system does not create an expectation of financial return.

The relationship between the user and the KOLO Foundation is a contractual relationship governed by the terms of use. The user is not a client purchasing a service, but a participant who voluntarily accepts the rules of a shared system.

### Article 3 — Nature of the accounting units

POEN is an internal accounting unit of the system by which contribution and other forms of participation in the common good are recorded in the KOLO Protocol's ledger. POEN is not money, currency, token, means of payment, electronic money, or digital asset.

ZRNO is an internal accounting unit of the system by which the user's position in the common good is recorded in the Protocol's ledger. ZRNO does not represent a share, equity, security, collective investment unit, investment contract, or digital asset. ZRNO carries no dividend, interest, return, or right to a liquidation surplus.

POEN and ZRNO have no value outside the system and cannot be converted into money, currency, or any instrument with value outside the system, directly or indirectly, including exchange for vouchers, gift cards, or other instruments with external value. The Foundation does not redeem POEN or ZRNO.

Users have no property right over POEN and ZRNO records in the system's ledger. A POEN record is data in the common-good ledger, not an asset owned by the user. ZRNO is a record of position in the common good, not a form of property right. Records may not be transferred to another person.

Recorded contribution does not constitute a claim against the Foundation.

### Article 4 — Risks of the accounting framework

The accounting coefficient is the numerical ratio of the total number of POEN recorded to the number of ZRNO available for registration in the Protocol. The accounting coefficient is an administrative figure — it is not a price, exchange rate, or performance index.

The accounting coefficient changes with activity in the system. No user, no institution, and no actor in the system controls the coefficient. Its rise or fall is a consequence of the activity of the entire community, not of any individual's decision.

A change in the position of a ZRNO Holder within the accounting system is not a return and is not guaranteed. The difference between the accounting coefficient at the time of ZRNO registration and at the time of deregistration is an arithmetic consequence of changes in the state of the entire system's ledger. No actor pays or guarantees that difference. Any benefit arising from a change in position is realised solely in POEN — records in the ledger with no external property value.

### Article 5 — Irrevocability of donations

Dinar funds that the user gives to the Foundation are donations within the meaning of the applicable regulations. A donation is irrevocable, regardless of the further development of the system.

A donor does not acquire a right to a refund, does not acquire a governance right in the Foundation by virtue of the donation, and does not acquire a share in the system by virtue of the donation.

The Protocol records the fact of the donor's contribution in POEN, but that record is not consideration for the donation. The donor may not make the donation conditional on the recording, nor does the recording create an obligation of the Foundation towards the donor.

### Article 6 — Public nature of the pseudonymous ledger

The contribution ledger is public in pseudonymous form. Pseudonymity is not anonymity — pseudonymised data remain personal data within the meaning of the Law on Personal Data Protection because they can, with additional information, be connected to an identified person.

The public nature of the pseudonymous contribution ledger is a structural characteristic of the system, not an option the user can disable. A user who does not accept this condition may not use the platform.

Even with pseudonymity, the combination of amounts, timing, and frequency of transactions may in certain cases enable indirect identification of the user. The user is aware of this limitation and accepts it by joining the system.

### Article 7 — Risks to system operation

The Foundation does not guarantee that the system will operate without interruption, that the infrastructure will not be affected by technical problems, or that the ledger will be protected against every security incident. The Foundation is obligated to take reasonable technical and organisational protective measures.

The system may be affected by technical problems, security incidents, or regulatory changes that affect its operation.

### Article 8 — Risk of Foundation cessation

The Foundation may cease to exist. In that case, the role of guardian of the common good and responsibility for the infrastructure pass to the legal successor in accordance with the Statute, but the continuity of the system's operation is not guaranteed.

### Article 9 — User responsibility in exchange

For the performance, quality, and risk in the exchange of goods and services, the users participating in such exchange are responsible in accordance with the general rules of the law of obligations. The Foundation does not mediate in exchange and is not a party to the legal relationship between users who exchange goods and services.

The user is responsible for the accuracy of the data they provide to the system. A user participating in the vouching chain is responsible for the truthfulness of their confirmation of reality in accordance with the rules on proof of reality.

### Article 10 — Tax risk

The system's position is that POEN and ZRNO are records without property value and that the exchange of goods and services within the system redistributes existing records without creating a monetary flow.

Tax authorities may take a different position. The exchange of goods and services within the system may be qualified as barter or another taxable event within the meaning of tax regulations, including regulations on personal income tax and value added tax.

The Foundation does not provide tax advice and cannot guarantee the tax treatment of users' activities in the system. The user is responsible for their own tax obligations that may arise in connection with participation in the system and is advised to consult a tax adviser.

### Article 11 — Regulatory risk

The KOLO system combines legal concepts from several existing categories. Serbian law has no ready-made legal category for systems of this type. The legal qualification of the system derives from its structural characteristics, in particular from the four principles established by the KOLO System Rulebook: non-convertibility, absence of property rights over records, irrevocability of donations, and data minimisation.

A change in the regulatory framework or in the interpretation of competent authorities may affect the operation of the system or the legal position of users.

### Article 12 — User declaration

By joining the KOLO system, the user declares:

— that they have read and understood this Statement, the Terms of Use, the Privacy Policy, and the system rules;

— that they understand the nature of POEN and ZRNO as internal accounting units with no value outside the system;

— that they understand that participation in the system is not an investment and that there is no expectation of financial return;

— that they understand that donations to the Foundation are irrevocable;

— that they understand the risks to system operation, including the possibility of technical problems, security incidents, and regulatory changes;

— that they understand that the Foundation does not guarantee the continuity of the system's operation;

— that they understand that the exchange of goods and services within the system may have tax implications and that they are responsible for their own tax obligations;

— that they accept the public nature of the pseudonymous ledger as a structural characteristic of the system;

— that they join the system voluntarily, on the basis of their own assessment and at their own responsibility.

### Article 13 — Amendments to the Statement

This Statement may be amended by a decision of the Management Board (UO) of the KOLO Foundation. Each amendment is communicated to users before it takes effect, within a period that allows them to become acquainted with the changes.

Continued use of the system after the amended Statement takes effect shall be deemed acceptance of the amendments.

### Article 14 — Entry into force

This Statement enters into force on the date of its adoption by the Management Board (UO) of the KOLO Foundation.

# Rulebook on Operational Contribution

*Version 3.7.2*

*This Rulebook governs the operational mechanics of operational contribution — the process of applying for, performing, and verifying off-platform activities whose contribution is recorded in POEN. It is adopted on the basis of Article 36 of the KOLO System Rulebook.*

## I — General Provisions

### Article 1

*Subject matter*

This Rulebook governs: the concept and content of a task for the common good, the persons authorised to propose a task, the procedure for a user to apply for execution, the content of an execution plan, the procedure for receiving an application, the procedure for execution and verification, the recording of POEN and distribution within the daily limit, transparency towards system users, and the termination of a contributor's participation.

Terms not defined in this Rulebook shall have the meaning established in the KOLO System Rulebook.

### Article 2

*Relationship with the KOLO System Rulebook*

This Rulebook elaborates the provisions of Article 36 of the KOLO System Rulebook. In the event of any inconsistency, the provisions of the KOLO System Rulebook shall prevail.

### Article 3

*Definitions*

The following terms are used in this Rulebook:

**Task for the common good (task)** — an off-platform activity that contributes to the common good, published for execution through the operational contribution channel.

**Task proposer** — a person or body authorised to publish a task on the platform in accordance with Article 4 of this Rulebook.

**Contributor** — a verified user who has applied for the execution of a task and whose application has been received in accordance with Chapter IV of this Rulebook.

**Execution plan** — a description of the manner, timing, and steps by which the contributor intends to perform the task.

**Verifier** — a person or body authorised to confirm the execution of a task and, in tasks with approval, to approve the execution plan.

**Daily execution** — a segment of work on a task performed within a single accounting period, subject to individual verification.

**Proposed POEN** — the number of POEN stated by the proposer alongside the task as a weighting coefficient for the distribution of the daily limit in accordance with Chapter VI of this Rulebook.

**Daily limit** — the maximum total POEN that may be recorded on account of operational contribution within one accounting period.

## II — Task for the Common Good

### Article 4

*Persons authorised to propose a task*

Tasks may be published by:

- the Foundation, through the Management Board (UO) or a person authorised by it;

- Gornje Kolo (Upper Kolo), by a decision adopted in accordance with the KOLO System Rulebook, upon its activation;

- a ZRNO Holder, independently, following the activation of the Upper Kolo.

In Phase 1, while the Upper Kolo has not yet been activated, tasks are published exclusively by the Foundation.

### Article 5

*Content of a task*

A task shall contain at least the following elements:

- the name and description of the task;

- the objective of the task and its contribution to the common good;

- execution criteria on the basis of which it is established that the task has been completed;

- an estimate of the scope of work;

- the proposed POEN for full execution;

- the application deadline and the execution deadline;

- the number of contributors to be accepted for the task;

- a designation of the task as a task "with approval" if Article 14 of this Rulebook applies;

- a qualification filter if Art. 9 paragraph 3 of this Rulebook applies.

### Article 6

*Proposed POEN*

The proposed POEN is determined according to the estimated scope of work and the nature of the task. The task proposer provides a statement of reasons alongside the proposed amount.

The proposed POEN is not a guaranteed amount that the contributor will receive. The actual ledger entry depends on the distribution of the daily limit in accordance with Chapter VI of this Rulebook.

Indicative rules for proposing POEN — time equivalents, types of work, upper values — may be established by a decision of the Management Board (UO) in Phase 1 and by the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

### Article 7

*Publication and status of a task*

Tasks are published on the platform.

A task has one of the following statuses: open, in execution, completed, not completed, or withdrawn.

The task content described in Article 5 of this Rulebook and the current status of the task are available to all system users, including unverified users.

### Article 8

*Amendment and withdrawal of a task*

The proposer may amend, supplement, or withdraw a task before the first application has been received, without providing reasons.

After the first application has been received, the task may be amended only with the consent of all contributors who are at that time accepted for the task. Withdrawal of a task in execution is possible only for justified reasons, which shall be stated publicly; daily executions by contributors already confirmed at the time of withdrawal remain recorded in accordance with Chapter VI.

## III — Application for Execution

### Article 9

*Persons eligible to apply*

Any verified user with a proof-of-reality index of at least 10% may apply for the execution of a task.

Unverified users and verified users whose index is below 10% may view tasks but may not submit an application for execution.

The task proposer may establish a higher proof-of-reality index threshold, previously completed tasks of a specified type, or other qualification requirements in the task if the nature of the work so requires. The qualification filter must be explained and must be proportionate to the nature of the task.

### Article 10

*Content of an application*

An application is submitted electronically on the platform and contains:

- the user's pseudonym;

- the execution plan referred to in Article 11 of this Rulebook;

- a declaration that the user is aware of the legal nature of operational contribution as set out in Article 28 of this Rulebook and of the weighted nature of the proposed POEN as set out in Article 22 of this Rulebook.

### Article 11

*Execution plan*

The execution plan contains:

- a description of how the user intends to perform the task;

- a timeline for execution, broken down by accounting periods or logical steps;

- the method of proving execution (photograph, report, link, other attachment);

- the proposed distribution of the proposed POEN across daily executions, if the task spans multiple days.

The user proposes the execution plan independently. The task proposer and the verifier do not interfere with the choice of working method; they express their view solely on whether the proposed plan, or the execution, satisfies the task criteria.

### Article 12

*Availability of applications*

Applications, execution plans, and the pseudonym of the contributor are available to all verified users of the system.

This ensures transparency towards the community and the possibility of filing an objection in accordance with Article 21 of this Rulebook.

## IV — Receipt of Application

### Article 13

*Automatic receipt*

Receipt of an application is an automatic act of the Protocol. A verified user who meets the requirements of Article 9 of this Rulebook and any qualification filter set out in the task becomes a contributor upon submitting an application. The task status changes to in execution.

For tasks with multiple intended contributors, the Protocol receives applications in the order of submission until the number specified in the task is reached. Subsequent applications are rejected automatically, with notification to the user.

During the course of a task, the verifier may issue a warning to a contributor that the execution plan as submitted is likely not to be confirmed, with reasons. The warning does not halt execution and does not change the task status; the contributor continues in all respects at their own risk.

### Article 14

*Tasks with approval*

The proposer may designate a task as a task "with approval" at the time of publication if there is a justified reason for doing so, in particular where:

- the work is specialised and requires verification of the contributor's qualifications in the specific case;

- the task carries an increased risk to the reputation of the Foundation or the community;

- the task's proposed POEN exceeds a threshold established by a decision of the Management Board (UO) in Phase 1, or by the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

The Management Board (UO) in Phase 1, or the Upper Kolo in Phase 2, may by its decision transfer an individual task to the approval regime after its publication, with reasons, if one of the grounds referred to in the preceding paragraph exists.

For tasks with approval, an application is not received until the verifier expressly approves the execution plan. The verifier shall express a view within seven days of the submission of the application. Rejection of an application must be reasoned; the user may submit an amended application for the same task.

The verifier approves an application if the execution plan corresponds to the nature and objective of the task, contains all elements listed in Article 11, and does not constitute an obvious abuse of the operational contribution channel.

### Article 15

*Multiple applications for the same task*

Where no qualification filter has been established in the task to distinguish between applicants, places on the task are filled in the order of submission.

Where a qualification filter has been established, the first application that satisfies it is accepted; for tasks with approval, the application that best corresponds to the qualification filter is accepted, with written reasons from the verifier.

### Article 16

*Verifier*

The execution plan in tasks with approval is approved, and execution in all tasks is verified, by:

- in Phase 1 — members of the Management Board (UO) of the Foundation or a person authorised by the Management Board;

- in Phase 2 — ZRNO Holders, in accordance with the operating rules of the Upper Kolo.

The verifier may not be the proposer of the same task if that would give rise to a conflict of interest. The verifier may not be a contributor to the same task.

## V — Execution and Verification

### Article 17

*Execution*

The contributor performs the task in accordance with the execution plan from their application. The contributor determines the method of execution independently; the proposer and verifier do not impose working instructions.

The contributor may amend the execution plan during execution, with prior notice to the verifier. A material amendment to the plan requires re-approval only in tasks with approval.

### Article 18

*Daily execution and verification*

For multi-day tasks, the contributor submits proof of daily execution on the platform at the end of each accounting period in which they worked.

The verifier shall express a view on daily execution within three accounting periods of the submission of proof. The verifier shall:

- confirm the daily execution, whereby the corresponding portion of the proposed POEN is introduced into the distribution for the accounting period in which the confirmation is issued, in accordance with Chapter VI of this Rulebook;

- reject it, with reasons, whereby no POEN is recorded for that daily execution;

- conditionally confirm it, requesting supplementary proof, whereby the decision is deferred pending the supplement.

### Article 19

*Final verification*

Upon notification of the completion of a task, the verifier shall express a view on the entire execution within seven days.

If the task has been executed in accordance with the criteria set out in the task, the verifier confirms execution and the remaining portion of the proposed POEN is introduced into the distribution for the accounting period in which the final confirmation is issued. If the task has not been fully executed, the verifier may confirm partial execution with proportionate distribution, or reject confirmation with reasons.

### Article 20

*Withdrawal by the contributor*

The contributor may withdraw from the task at any time by an electronic declaration on the platform, without reasons and without any consequences other than the absence of a ledger entry for the uncompleted portion of the task.

Verified daily executions up to the time of withdrawal remain in the distribution for the accounting periods in which they were confirmed. The task reverts to open status, unless the task proposer decides to withdraw it in accordance with Article 8 of this Rulebook.

### Article 21

*Objection*

Any verified user may, during the course of a task, submit a reasoned objection to an application, execution plan, daily verification, or final verification. The objection is available to all verified users.

The verifier shall express a view on the objection within seven days. An objection does not in itself halt execution, but the Management Board (UO) in Phase 1, or the Upper Kolo in Phase 2, may by decision temporarily suspend further verification and distribution under that task until the objection is resolved.

## VI — Recording of POEN and Distribution

### Article 22

*Weighted nature of the proposed POEN*

The proposed POEN is not a guaranteed amount that the contributor receives upon verification of execution. The proposed POEN is a weighting coefficient on the basis of which the contributor is allocated a proportionate share of the daily limit of the accounting period in which the verification is issued.

All confirmed verifications within an accounting period enter into the distribution for that period. No confirmed verification is deferred to the next period, and none creates an obligation of the system towards the contributor.

### Article 23

*Daily limit*

The maximum total POEN that may be recorded on account of operational contribution within one accounting period equals 10% of the total POEN recorded in the system at the start of that accounting period.

The daily limit is hard and may not be exceeded. The distribution in accordance with Article 24 of this Rulebook ensures that the total of all entries on account of operational contribution in one accounting period does not exceed the daily limit.

### Article 24

*Distribution formula*

At the end of each accounting period the Protocol performs the distribution calculation as a single act for all verifications confirmed in that period.

Let P denote the sum of proposed POEN of all verifications confirmed in the current accounting period, and L the daily limit referred to in Article 23 of this Rulebook. The amount of POEN recorded for an individual confirmed verification is calculated as:

*recorded POEN = proposed POEN × min(1, L/P)*

If P is less than or equal to L, each confirmed verification is credited its full proposed POEN. If P is greater than L, each confirmed verification is credited a proportionate share corresponding to its weight in the total sum of proposed POEN.

Any amount not recorded due to the daily limit being exceeded is not carried over to the next accounting period and does not create an obligation of the system towards the contributor.

### Article 25

*Moment of recording*

The recording of POEN on account of operational contribution is an automatic act of the Protocol executed at the end of the accounting period, simultaneously with the calculation of the accounting coefficient.

POEN are entered in the contributor's record and have the same status as all other POEN in the system — they are non-convertible, have no external value, and confer no property right on the user.

### Article 26

*Upper limit of proposed POEN per task*

The proposed POEN for a single task, as well as the proposed POEN for an individual daily execution in a multi-day task, may not exceed the upper limit established by a decision of the Management Board (UO) in Phase 1, or by the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

The upper limit is expressed as an absolute amount or as a percentage of the current daily limit. The purpose of this limit is to prevent a single task or a single verification from monopolising the entire daily distribution.

### Article 27

*Variability of limits*

The percentage referred to in Article 23 of this Rulebook and the upper limit referred to in Article 26 of this Rulebook are operational parameters of the system.

Their values are changed by the Management Board (UO) of the Foundation in Phase 1, or by the Upper Kolo in Phase 2. A change takes effect from the first accounting period following the adoption of the decision and is communicated to users in accordance with the KOLO System Rulebook.

## VII — Legal Qualification

### Article 28

*Operational contribution and employment*

Operational contribution does not establish an employment relationship within the meaning of Article 5 of the Labour Act. In the relationship between the task proposer, the verifier, and the contributor:

- there is no subordination — the contributor independently decides whether to apply, independently proposes the method of execution, independently determines the pace of work, and may withdraw at any time without consequences;

- there is no personal obligation to work — accepting a task is voluntary and does not create a legal obligation;

- there is no remuneration — POEN recorded following verified execution are entries in the Protocol's ledger with no external property value.

POEN recorded on account of operational contribution do not constitute remuneration within the meaning of labour regulations, personal income tax regulations, or payment services regulations.

The proposed POEN is not agreed-upon consideration for work — it is a weighting coefficient in the distribution of the shared daily limit, whose actual realisation depends on the state of the system and total demand in the accounting period, in accordance with Chapter VI of this Rulebook.

## VIII — Transparency

### Article 29

*Data available to all users*

The following data on operational contribution are available to all system users, including unverified users:

- published tasks with all elements listed in Article 5 of this Rulebook;

- the current status of each task.

### Article 30

*Data available to verified users*

The following data are available exclusively to verified system users:

- applications and execution plans;

- the pseudonym of the contributor and the verifier;

- the verifier's decisions on receipt of an application in tasks with approval, on daily execution, and on final execution, with reasons;

- POEN recorded per task and per contributor;

- objections and their outcomes;

- the current usage of the daily limit and the distribution coefficient for the current accounting period, as well as historical distribution data for previous accounting periods.

### Article 31

*Pseudonymity*

Data on the contributor and the verifier are published under their pseudonyms in the system. The personal data of the contributor and the verifier are not published through the operational contribution channel.

## IX — Final Provisions

### Article 32

*Amendments to the Rulebook*

This Rulebook is adopted and amended by the Management Board (UO) of the KOLO Foundation, under the procedure established in the KOLO System Rulebook.

### Article 33

*Entry into force*

This Rulebook enters into force on the date of its adoption by the Management Board (UO) of the KOLO Foundation.

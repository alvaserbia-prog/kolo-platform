# Rulebook on the Hierarchy of Acts of the KOLO System

*Version 3.7.2*

## I — Introductory Provisions

### Article 1 — Subject matter

(1) This Rulebook governs the system of acts of the KOLO system, their mutual rank, the rules for resolving inconsistencies between acts, and the competence and limits for their adoption and amendment.

(2) The purpose of this Rulebook is to ensure the unity, predictability, and internal consistency of all acts of the KOLO system.

### Article 2 — Position of this Rulebook

(1) This Rulebook is adopted on the basis of the Statute of the KOLO Foundation (hereinafter: the Statute) and is subordinate to the Statute.

(2) This Rulebook is superior to the KOLO Rulebook and to all other rulebooks of the KOLO system.

(3) The rank and content of this Rulebook may not be altered by an act of lower rank.

### Article 3 — Conceptual layer

(1) The KOLO System Whitepaper is a conceptual and public document describing the purpose, architecture, and principles of the system.

(2) The Whitepaper is not a binding act, does not create rights or obligations, and does not enter into the hierarchy set out in this Rulebook.

(3) In the event of a discrepancy between the Whitepaper and any binding act, the binding act shall prevail.

## II — System and Rank of Acts

### Article 4 — Binding acts of the KOLO system

The binding acts of the KOLO system are:

- the Statute;

- this Rulebook;

- the KOLO Rulebook;

- other rulebooks;

- personal data protection acts;

- platform acts;

- forms and internal acts.

### Article 5 — Hierarchy of acts

(1) The acts referred to in Art. 4 points 1) to 4) of this Rulebook form a hierarchical order of precedence as follows:

- the Statute — the supreme internal act;

- this Rulebook;

- the KOLO Rulebook;

- other rulebooks.

(2) An act of higher rank is superior to every act of lower rank.

(3) Personal data protection acts, platform acts, and forms and internal acts do not enter into the hierarchical order referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article; they apply in parallel, in the manner governed by Art. 9 to 11 of this Rulebook.

### Article 6 — KOLO Rulebook

(1) The KOLO Rulebook is the overarching operational act of the KOLO system; it governs the organisation, functioning, and internal rules of the system.

(2) The KOLO Rulebook is subordinate to the Statute and to this Rulebook, and is superior to all other rulebooks.

### Article 7 — Other rulebooks

(1) Other rulebooks elaborate specific matters within the framework established by the KOLO Rulebook; each bears its own name according to its subject matter.

(2) The other rulebooks of the KOLO system are:

- Rulebook on Proof of Reality;

- Rulebook on Founding Contribution;

- Rulebook on Sponsorship and Donations;

- Rulebook on Operational Contribution;

- Rulebook on Circles and Cooperatives;

- Support Programme rulebooks;

- Rulebook on the Children's Module;

- Rulebook on Disputes.

(3) Other rulebooks are mutually equal in rank and are delineated by subject matter.

(4) In addition to the rulebooks listed in paragraph 2 of this Article, other rulebooks may be adopted when the KOLO Rulebook expressly refers the regulation of a specific matter to a separate rulebook; such a rulebook has the rank of other rulebooks.

### Article 8 — Rule on conflict of acts

(1) In the event of inconsistency between provisions of acts of different rank, the act of higher rank shall apply.

(2) An act of lower rank may not restrict, amend, or repeal what is governed by an act of higher rank, nor govern a specific matter in a manner contrary to it.

(3) A provision of an act of lower rank that conflicts with an act of higher rank shall not apply to the extent of the conflict; the remainder of the act remains in force.

(4) An inconsistency between two rulebooks of the same rank is resolved by applying the rulebook whose subject matter is more narrowly and directly related to the matter being regulated.

### Article 9 — Personal data protection acts

(1) Personal data protection acts are the Privacy Policy, the Record of Processing Activities, the Data Protection Impact Assessment, and the Decision on the Appointment of the Data Protection Officer.

(2) These acts govern the processing of personal data in the KOLO system and apply in parallel with other acts, supplementing them within their domain.

(3) In matters of personal data processing, the acts referred to in paragraph 1 of this Article and the personal data protection regulations shall prevail; no other act of the KOLO system may govern the processing of personal data in a manner contrary to them.

### Article 10 — Platform acts

(1) Platform acts are the Terms of Use and the Risk Acceptance Statement; they govern the relationship with the platform user.

(2) Platform acts must be consistent with the acts in the hierarchical order and may not restrict or deny rights recognised to the user by those acts.

(3) In the event of inconsistency between a platform act and the KOLO Rulebook or any other rulebook, the solution more favourable to the user shall apply.

(4) Mandatory consumer protection provisions and provisions protecting the weaker contracting party apply directly and remain unaffected by the provisions of platform acts.

### Article 11 — Forms and internal acts

(1) Forms and internal acts are: donation agreement templates, the cooperative collaboration agreement template, the Management Board (UO) Rules of Procedure, the Director's Value Limit Decision, and other acts of an individual or executive nature.

(2) Forms and internal acts implement the acts in the hierarchical order and must be consistent with them.

(3) Forms and internal acts may not introduce general rules or govern matters reserved for the KOLO Rulebook or other rulebooks.

## III — Adoption and Amendment of Acts

### Article 12 — Adoption and amendment of acts

(1) The Statute is adopted and amended under the procedure prescribed by the Statute and the Law on Endowments and Foundations.

(2) This Rulebook, in all phases of the system, is adopted and amended by the Foundation, under the procedure prescribed for internal acts adopted by its Management Board (UO).

(3) In Phase 1, the KOLO Rulebook and other rulebooks are adopted and amended by the Foundation; the founder's powers in that phase are established by the Statute and the KOLO Rulebook.

(4) Upon the system's transition to Phase 2, amendments to the KOLO Rulebook and other rulebooks in the part relating to protocol rules, modules, and common-good matters are adopted under the procedure and within the competence established by the KOLO Rulebook, subject to the Foundation's protective veto for as long as it subsists; in the remaining part, the KOLO Rulebook and other rulebooks are amended by the Foundation within its statutory powers.

(5) Personal data protection acts, platform acts, and forms and internal acts are adopted and amended within the competence of the Foundation, in accordance with the applicable regulations and acts of higher rank.

### Article 13 — Limits on amendments

No amendment to the acts of the KOLO system, including a decision adopted in Phase 2, may derogate or restrict the structural principles of the KOLO system established by the KOLO Rulebook.

### Article 14 — Entry into force

This Rulebook enters into force [on the date of adoption / on the eighth day following the date of adoption].

# Statute of the KOLO Foundation

*Version 3.7.2*

In accordance with the provisions of Articles 33, paragraph 4, and 34 of the Law on Endowments and Foundations ("Official Gazette of the Republic of Serbia", No. 88/2010, 99/2011 — another law, and 44/2018 — another law), the Management Board, at its session held on 16 May 2026 in Sombor, adopted the following:

## I — Introductory Provisions

### Article 1.

The KOLO Foundation (hereinafter: the Foundation) is a private legal entity without members, established for an indefinite period in order to pursue the general-interest objectives established in the founding act and in this Statute.

The Foundation has legal personality and is liable for obligations undertaken in legal transactions with all of its assets.

### Article 2.

The name of the Foundation is: "КОЛО Фондација".

The name of the Foundation in English is: "KOLO Foundation".

### Article 3.

The registered office of the Foundation is in Sombor, Šetalište 16.

### Article 4.

The Foundation has a round seal bearing the name "KOLO FONDACIJA" in the upper part, the name of the registered office in the lower part, and the Foundation's emblem in the centre.

### Article 5.

The Foundation's emblem is a stylised graphic depiction of three human figures arranged in a circle with their arms forming a ring, with an ear of wheat at the centre.

## II — Objectives and Activities

### Article 6.

The objectives of the Foundation are:

— the development of the social and solidarity economy and alternative economic models based on the principles of mutuality, voluntary cooperation, democratic decision-making, and the primacy of social purpose over capital;

— the recording and recognition of the contributions of individuals and collective forms of action to general-interest objectives and the common good;

— support for the voluntary association of citizens for the purposes of mutual aid and self-organisation;

— social protection and solidarity support for vulnerable social groups;

— education and the popularisation of knowledge about the social and solidarity economy, mutuality, and alternative economic models.

### Article 7.

The Foundation pursues its objectives in particular through:

a) the development and maintenance of software infrastructure and related services that support the achievement of the Foundation's objectives, in accordance with the Foundation's rulebooks;

b) the implementation of designated-use programmes and mutual aid programmes;

c) the organisation of collective procurement of goods and services for the benefit of programme beneficiaries;

d) investment in infrastructure serving programme beneficiaries and the Foundation's activities;

e) financial, organisational, and professional support for collective forms of association, including cooperatives, associations, and other forms of citizen self-organisation operating in accordance with the principles of the social and solidarity economy;

f) the provision of solidarity assistance in emergency situations;

g) educational activities, seminars, workshops, and the publication of materials;

h) cooperation with state institutions, kindred organisations, associations, cooperatives, and other entities with the same or similar objectives.

## III — Foundation Assets

### Article 8.

The Foundation acquires assets from voluntary contributions, donations, sponsorships, legacies, income from assets, and other sources in accordance with the law.

### Article 9.

The Foundation's assets are used exclusively for the pursuit of the objectives established in the founding act and in this Statute.

The Foundation's assets may not be distributed to founders, members of governing bodies, employees, or persons connected with them, except for the payment of reasonable remuneration and compensation, reimbursement of expenses, fulfilment of contractual obligations, and the payment of employees' salaries.

The distribution of funds and assets to beneficiaries is carried out exclusively through the programmes referred to in Article 7 of this Statute.

The beneficiaries of the Foundation's programmes are natural persons and collective forms of citizen association who participate in the Foundation's programmes under the conditions established in the Foundation's rulebooks, as well as vulnerable social groups to whom the Foundation's solidarity support is directed.

## IV — Foundation Bodies

### Article 10.

The bodies of the Foundation are the Management Board (UO) and the Director.

### Article 11.

The Management Board (UO) manages the Foundation and has three members.

The term of office of Management Board members is four years, with the possibility of re-election.

The founder may be a member of the Management Board.

### Article 12.

The Management Board (UO):

1. adopts and amends the Statute and other general acts of the Foundation;

2. appoints and dismisses the Director;

3. adopts the financial plan and approves the annual accounts;

4. decides on the use of the Foundation's assets;

5. adopts rulebooks governing programmes, activities, internal organisation, and other matters of significance for the Foundation's work;

6. ensures the transparency of the Foundation's work;

7. decides on changes to the name and registered office of the Foundation;

8. decides on structural changes and the dissolution of the Foundation;

9. performs other functions in accordance with the law and this Statute.

The Management Board (UO) may by rulebook establish advisory or other bodies that participate in the preparation of draft decisions, as well as mechanisms for collecting the views of the Foundation's service users.

### Article 13.

The President of the Management Board (UO) convenes and chairs sessions. In their absence, sessions are chaired by a member designated by the Management Board.

The Management Board (UO) validly decides if a majority of the total number of members is present.

The Management Board (UO) decides by a majority of the total number of members.

For amendments to the Statute, structural changes, and the dissolution of the Foundation, a two-thirds majority of the total number of members is required.

The Director does not have the right to vote on decisions concerning their own appointment and dismissal. When deciding on those matters, the required majority is calculated on the basis of the number of members entitled to vote.

The Management Board (UO) may also make decisions by electronic means, without holding a session. The manner of electronic decision-making is established by the Management Board's Rules of Procedure.

### Article 14.

The President and members of the first Management Board are appointed by the founder.

In subsequent terms, the Management Board (UO) appoints new members no later than thirty days before the expiry of the term of office.

A member whose term has expired continues to perform their duties until a new member is appointed, but for no longer than ninety days from the expiry of the term.

If no new member is appointed within the period referred to in the preceding paragraph, the founder shall make the appointment. If the founder does not exist or does not make a decision within a reasonable time, the competent authority shall decide on the appointment in accordance with the law.

### Article 15.

Membership of the Management Board (UO) ceases upon expiry of the term of office, removal, loss of legal capacity, resignation, or death.

A member of the Management Board is removed by the Management Board by a two-thirds majority vote, with the person whose removal is being voted on having no right to vote.

### Article 16.

A member of the Management Board (UO) and the Director may resign by written notice addressed to the Management Board. A resignation takes effect on the expiry of the tenth day from the receipt of the written notice.

### Article 17.

A vacant seat on the Management Board (UO) is filled in the manner prescribed for appointment. The term of the new member runs until the end of the predecessor's term.

### Article 18.

The Director represents the Foundation, is responsible for the lawfulness of its work, conducts the Foundation's affairs in accordance with the decisions of the Management Board (UO), and submits draft financial plans and annual accounts.

The Director may be a member of the Management Board (UO).

### Article 19.

The first Director is appointed by the founder in the founding act.

The Director is appointed and dismissed by the Management Board (UO). The Director's term of office is four years, with the possibility of re-election.

Before making a dismissal decision, the Management Board (UO) must give the Director an opportunity to present their position on the reasons for dismissal.

The Management Board (UO) dismisses the Director in particular if the Director does not act in good faith, uses the Foundation's assets in their personal interest, causes damage to the Foundation, or for other justified reasons determined by the Management Board.

## V — Founder

### Article 20.

The founder of the Foundation has the rights established by law and this Statute.

The founder appoints the first Management Board.

## VI — Transparency of Work

### Article 21.

The Foundation's work is public. Transparency is ensured by publishing the annual activity report, the annual financial report, and Management Board decisions of significance for users and the public, including decisions on the adoption and amendment of rulebooks, on the programmes referred to in Article 7 of this Statute, and decisions that materially affect users' rights.

Publication is carried out through the Foundation's website or by other appropriate means.

Any person has the right to inspect the Foundation's documents, in accordance with the law and the Foundation's acts, subject to the protection of personal data and trade secrets.

## VII — General Acts

### Article 22.

The general acts of the Foundation are rulebooks, rules of procedure of bodies, employment rules, and other acts governing matters of significance for the Foundation's work.

General acts of the Foundation are adopted and amended by the Management Board (UO) by a majority of the total number of members, on the proposal of the Director or a member of the Management Board.

General acts of the Foundation must be consistent with this Statute.

General acts take effect on the date specified therein, but no earlier than the date of publication on the Foundation's website.

## VIII — Statute Amendments

### Article 23.

An initiative to amend or supplement the Statute may be submitted by the founder, the Management Board (UO), or the Director.

The decision to amend or supplement the Statute is adopted by the Management Board (UO) by a two-thirds majority of the total number of members.

Amendments and supplements to the Statute take effect on the date of entry in the Register of Endowments and Foundations.

## IX — Structural Changes and Dissolution

### Article 24.

Decisions on the merger by absorption, merger by formation of a new entity, division, change of legal form, and dissolution of the Foundation are adopted by the Management Board (UO) by a two-thirds majority of the total number of members.

### Article 25.

In the event of dissolution, the remaining assets of the Foundation are allocated to another endowment, foundation, or association established for the pursuit of the same or similar general-interest objectives, with preference for those operating in accordance with the principles of the social and solidarity economy.

The distribution decision is adopted by the Management Board (UO) within thirty days of the dissolution decision. If the Management Board does not adopt a decision within the specified period, the decision on the distribution of the remaining assets shall be made by the competent authority of the local self-government unit in whose territory the Foundation's registered office is located.

## X — Final Provisions

### Article 26.

The Management Board (UO) shall adopt the rulebooks provided for in this Statute within sixty days of its entry into force.

### Article 27.

Matters not regulated by this Statute shall be governed by the Law on Endowments and Foundations.

### Article 28.

This Statute enters into force on the date of entry in the Register of Endowments and Foundations.

In Sombor, on 16 May 2026.

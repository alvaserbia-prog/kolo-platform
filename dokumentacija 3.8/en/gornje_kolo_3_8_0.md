# Rulebook on the Upper Kolo

*Version 3.8.0*

Date of preparation: 03.06.2026.

*This Rulebook governs the voting procedures and operational rules of the Upper Kolo. It is adopted on the basis of Articles 41, 46, and 47 of the KOLO System Rulebook (v3.8.0), which expressly refer to this act. In the event of inconsistency, the provisions of the KOLO System Rulebook shall prevail.*

## I — General Provisions

### Article 1 — Subject matter

This Rulebook governs: the composition of the Upper Kolo and the determination of voting power, the decision-making procedure, the proposal and voting process, the delegation of votes, the execution of decisions and interaction with the Foundation, the transparency of operations and the decision register, and the procedure for amending this Rulebook.

Terms not defined by this Rulebook have the meaning established in the KOLO System Rulebook.

### Article 2 — Relationship with the KOLO System Rulebook

Chapter VII of the KOLO System Rulebook (Art. 41–51) governs the composition and competence of the Upper Kolo, quadratic voting as a principle, delegation as a principle, the Foundation's protective veto, and the limits of governance power. This Rulebook does not amend or repeat those provisions, but elaborates their operational application.

### Article 3 — Temporal applicability

The Upper Kolo comes into existence and becomes the governance body of the system in Phase 2, upon reaching the threshold of 1,000,000 recorded POENs (Art. 42, 44 of the Rulebook). The procedures in this Rulebook apply from the constitution of the Upper Kolo.

In Phase 1, the rules of the Protocol are established by the founder in cooperation with the Foundation (Art. 42, 43 of the Rulebook); the provisions of this Rulebook on voting do not apply until the Upper Kolo has been established.

## II — Composition and Voting Power

### Article 4 — Composition

The Upper Kolo consists of all ZRNO Holders. The right to participate in decision-making is exercised exclusively through activated ZRNO.

### Article 5 — Voting power

The voting power of a holder equals the floor of the square root of their active ZRNO: power = ⌊√(active ZRNO)⌋.

Free ZRNO does not confer voting power. By activating ZRNO, a holder acquires voting power but forfeits the ability to redeem that ZRNO until it is returned to the free state (Art. 46 of the Rulebook). ZRNO is not consumed by voting.

### Article 6 — Determination of voting power per accounting period

Voting power is determined per accounting period, not at every moment. Since the inscription, redemption, and locking of ZRNO (Chapter IV of the Rulebook) and the delegation of votes (Art. 47 of the Rulebook) take effect at midnight of the accounting period, the number of active ZRNO and delegated power are constant throughout the entire accounting period and change only at the boundary between periods.

A vote carries the voting power of the accounting period in which it is cast. A holder may change their vote while voting is open; a changed vote carries the voting power of the accounting period in which the change was made.

## III — Decision-Making

### Article 7 — Matters outside competence

No decision of the Upper Kolo may (Art. 50 of the Rulebook): abolish the non-convertibility of records, introduce a property right over records, make donations refundable, abandon the principle of data minimization, or replace the common-good licences (AGPL-3.0 and CC BY-SA 4.0) with more restrictive ones. These matters are not subject to a vote; a proposal of such content is inadmissible.

While the Foundation's protective veto is in force, it constitutes a limitation on the execution of decisions in accordance with Art. 48 of the Rulebook and Chapter VI of this Rulebook.

### Article 8 — Decision and decision-making procedure

The Upper Kolo decides on the rules of the Protocol, on the activation and deactivation of modules, and on other matters within its competence (Art. 45 of the Rulebook), except for the matters referred to in Article 7.

All decisions are adopted by a simple majority of votes cast.

A decision is adopted if the sum of the voting power of votes cast "for" exceeds the sum of the voting power of votes cast "against".

### Article 9 — Tied result

If the sum of the voting power of votes cast "for" equals the sum of the voting power of votes cast "against", the proposal is deemed not adopted and the existing state of affairs remains in force.

## IV — Procedure

### Article 10 — Proposing

Any holder with at least one active ZRNO may submit a proposal. A proposal contains a clear title and a statement of reasons sufficiently specific to enable decision-making.

A proposal requesting action outside the competence of the Upper Kolo (Art. 7) is inadmissible and is not put to a vote.

### Article 11 — Voting period

The voting period is the next accounting period. A proposal opened during one accounting period is put to a vote in the next accounting period and closes at the end of that period. The proposer does not set a deadline.

Since voting spans one accounting period, the number of active ZRNO and delegated power are constant during it, so all votes cast carry the voting power of the same accounting period (Art. 6).

Upon expiry of the period, voting closes automatically; subsequent votes are not possible. During the voting period (within that accounting period) a holder may change their vote.

### Article 12 — Voting

Voting is conducted by declaring "for" or "against". A vote is weighted by the holder's voting power determined in accordance with Articles 5 and 6.

A holder who has delegated their vote does not vote personally while the delegation is in force (Art. 14).

### Article 13 — Publication of the outcome

Upon the close of voting, the outcome is determined by aggregating the voting power of "for" and "against" votes and applying the rules in Article 8. The outcome (adopted / not adopted, with the voting-power totals) is publicly announced.

## V — Delegation of Votes

### Article 14 — General delegation

A ZRNO Holder may delegate their voting power to another ZRNO Holder. Delegation is general — the delegate votes on behalf of the delegator on all matters while the delegation is in force. Delegated voting power is added to the delegate's own power.

### Article 15 — Chain and prohibition of a circle

Delegation may be indirect: if the delegate also delegates their power, the delegated power passes along the chain to the holder who votes personally. A delegation that would form a circle (a closed chain in which no one votes personally) has no effect and the power within such a circle is not counted.

### Article 16 — Revocation

A delegation may be revoked at any time. A revocation and a new delegation take effect from midnight of the next accounting period, in order to ensure stability of power determination during open votings.

## VI — Execution of Decisions and the Foundation

### Article 17 — Execution

The Foundation, in its service role (Art. 51 of the Rulebook), executes an adopted decision of the Upper Kolo — through application via the Protocol or by an appropriate act, in accordance with the law.

### Article 18 — Protective veto and financial self-sufficiency

The Foundation may place a reasoned protective veto on the execution of a decision that would jeopardise the operational and financial sustainability of the Foundation before it achieves financial self-sufficiency (Art. 48 of the Rulebook). The veto is raised without delay upon the determination of the outcome, with a statement of reasons pointing to a specific threat to sustainability.

The veto suspends the execution of the decision. The Upper Kolo may amend the proposal so as to eliminate the identified threat and decide on it again.

A veto without a statement of reasons, or a veto that does not point to a specific threat to sustainability, constitutes an abuse and is subject to liability in accordance with the KOLO System Rulebook.

### Article 19 — Threshold of financial self-sufficiency and extinction of the veto

The protective veto is permanently and irreversibly extinguished (Art. 49 of the Rulebook) when the Foundation's liquid dinar funds reach at least three (3) times the Foundation's operational cost for the preceding month.

The operational cost for the preceding month is calculated from the Foundation's records. The threshold cannot be reached before at least one full month of operational cost records exists.

Extinction of the veto is irreversible and does not abolish the statutory obligations of the Management Board (Art. 49 of the Rulebook). The dinar funds balance, the recorded operational cost for the preceding month, and the calculated threshold are publicly available.

### Article 20 — Dinar funds

With regard to the allocation of dinar funds, the Upper Kolo makes recommendations to the Management Board of the Foundation (Art. 51 of the Rulebook). The Management Board considers the recommendation and applies it within its statutory powers, with a reasoned response to each recommendation.

## VII — Transparency and Decision Register

### Article 21

All proposals, the course of voting, and the outcomes are publicly visible to ZRNO Holders and, in accordance with the system's visibility rules, to other users. The Protocol maintains a decision register that contains the title and statement of reasons of the proposal, the proposer, the deadline, the outcome, and the voting-power totals "for" and "against". Entries in the register are not retroactively altered.

## VIII — Reconsideration and Amendment of the Rulebook

### Article 22 — Re-proposing

A proposal of the same or substantially identical content that was not adopted may not be put to a vote again before the expiry of thirty (30) days from the close of the preceding vote, in order to prevent overloading the system with repetition.

### Article 23 — Amendment of this Rulebook

This Rulebook is amended by a decision of the Upper Kolo (Art. 8). In Phase 1, before the establishment of the Upper Kolo, the Rulebook is adopted and amended by the founder in cooperation with the Foundation (Art. 43 of the Rulebook).

An amendment to this Rulebook may not be inconsistent with the KOLO System Rulebook, nor may it encroach on matters outside the competence of the Upper Kolo (Art. 7).

## IX — Constitution in Phase 2

### Article 24

Upon transition to Phase 2 (Art. 44 of the Rulebook), the Upper Kolo is constituted automatically — it consists of the holders who have inscribed ZRNO. From that moment, the procedures in this Rulebook apply. Until the first decisions of the Upper Kolo are adopted, the existing rules of the Protocol remain in force.

## X — Final Provisions

### Article 25 — Alignment with the Protocol

The voting period tied to the next accounting period (Art. 11) and the re-proposing period (Art. 22) apply in the Protocol from the constitution of the Upper Kolo. The existing voting implementation (quadratic voting power, changing a vote before the deadline, automatic closure, delegation with midnight effect) is aligned with this Rulebook: the voting deadline currently set by the proposer is replaced by binding to the next accounting period, the outcome is determined by a simple majority of votes cast and announced as a decision. No quorum applies.

### Article 26 — Entry into force

This Rulebook enters into force on the date of its adoption; the provisions on the voting procedure apply from the constitution of the Upper Kolo in Phase 2.

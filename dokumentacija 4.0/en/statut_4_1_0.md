# Statute of the KOLO Foundation

*Version 4.1*

*Adopted by the Board of Directors at the session held on 16.05.2026 in Sombor; entered into force upon entry in the Register of Endowments and Foundations (21.07.2026). KOLO Foundation — registration number 28836627, PIB 115840443.*

In accordance with the provisions of Articles 33 paragraph 4 and 34 of the Law on Endowments and Foundations ("Official Gazette of the Republic of Serbia" no. 88/2010, 99/2011 — other law and 44/2018 — other law), the Board of Directors, at the session held on 16.05.2026 in Sombor, adopted:

# STATUTE OF THE KOLO FOUNDATION

## I — INTRODUCTORY PROVISIONS

### Article 1.

The KOLO Foundation (hereinafter: the Foundation) is a private legal entity without members, established for an indefinite period for the purpose of achieving public-benefit objectives determined by the founding act and this Statute.

The Foundation has the status of a legal entity and is liable for obligations undertaken in legal transactions with its entire assets.

### Article 2.

The name of the Foundation is: "КОЛО Фондација".

The name of the Foundation in English is: "KOLO Foundation".

### Article 3.

The registered office of the Foundation is in Sombor, Šetalište 16.

### Article 4.

The Foundation has a round seal with the name "КОЛО ФОНДАЦИЈА" in the upper part, the name of the seat "СОМБОР" in the lower part, and the emblem of the Foundation in the middle.

### Article 5.

The emblem of the Foundation is a stylised graphic depiction of three human figures arranged in a circle with arms forming a ring, with an ear of wheat at the centre.

## II — OBJECTIVES AND ACTIVITIES

### Article 6.

The objectives of the Foundation are:

— development of the social and solidarity economy and alternative economic models based on the principles of mutuality, voluntary cooperation, democratic decision-making and the primacy of social purpose over capital;

— recording and recognition of the contributions of individuals and collective forms of action to public-benefit objectives and the common good;

— support for the voluntary association of citizens for the purpose of mutual aid and self-organisation;

— social protection and solidarity support for vulnerable social groups;

— education and popularisation of knowledge about the social and solidarity economy, mutuality and alternative economic models.

### Article 7.

The Foundation achieves its objectives in particular through:

a) development and maintenance of software infrastructure and accompanying services that support the achievement of the Foundation's objectives, in accordance with the Foundation's rulebooks;

b) implementation of programmes for the dedicated use of funds and mutual aid programmes;

c) organisation of collective procurement of goods and services for the benefit of programme beneficiaries;

d) investment in infrastructure serving programme beneficiaries and the Foundation's activities;

e) financial, organisational and expert support to collective forms of association, including cooperatives, associations and other forms of citizens' self-organisation acting in accordance with the principles of the social and solidarity economy;

f) provision of solidarity assistance in emergency situations;

g) educational activities, seminars, workshops and publishing;

h) cooperation with state institutions, kindred organisations, associations, cooperatives and other entities with the same or similar objectives.

## III — ASSETS OF THE FOUNDATION

### Article 8.

The Foundation acquires assets from voluntary contributions, donations, sponsorships, bequests, income from assets and other sources in accordance with the law.

### Article 9.

The assets of the Foundation are used exclusively for achieving the objectives determined by the founding act and this Statute.

The assets of the Foundation may not be distributed to the founders, members of its bodies, employees or persons related to them, except for the payment of appropriate awards and remuneration, reimbursement of expenses, fulfilment of contractual obligations and payment of employees' salaries.

The distribution of funds and goods to beneficiaries is carried out exclusively through the programmes referred to in Article 7 of this Statute.

The beneficiaries of the Foundation's programmes are natural persons and collective forms of citizens' association participating in the Foundation's programmes under the conditions determined by the Foundation's rulebooks, as well as vulnerable social groups to whom the Foundation's solidarity support is directed.

## IV — BODIES OF THE FOUNDATION

### Article 10.

The bodies of the Foundation are the Board of Directors and the Director.

### Article 11.

The Board of Directors manages the Foundation and has three members.

The mandate of the members of the Board of Directors lasts four years, with the possibility of re-election.

The founder may be a member of the Board of Directors.

### Article 12.

The Board of Directors:

1\. adopts and amends the Statute and other general acts of the Foundation;

2\. appoints and dismisses the Director;

3\. adopts the financial plan and approves the annual accounts;

4\. decides on the use of the Foundation's assets;

5\. adopts rulebooks governing programmes, activities, internal organisation and other matters of importance for the Foundation's work;

6\. ensures the transparency of the Foundation's work;

7\. decides on changes to the name and registered office of the Foundation;

8\. decides on status changes and the termination of the Foundation;

9\. performs other tasks in accordance with the law and this Statute.

The Board of Directors may, by rulebook, establish advisory or other bodies participating in the preparation of draft decisions, as well as mechanisms for collecting the opinions of the users of the Foundation's services.

### Article 13.

The President of the Board of Directors convenes and chairs the sessions. In their absence, sessions are chaired by a member designated by the Board of Directors.

The Board of Directors validly decides if a majority of the total number of members is present.

The Board of Directors decides by a majority of votes of the total number of members.

Amendments to the Statute, status changes and the termination of the Foundation require a two-thirds majority of votes of the total number of members.

The Director has no voting right when deciding on their own appointment and dismissal. When deciding on those matters, the required majority is calculated from the number of members entitled to vote.

The Board of Directors may also adopt decisions electronically, without holding a session. The manner of electronic decision-making is determined by the rules of procedure of the Board of Directors.

### Article 14.

The President and members of the first convocation of the Board of Directors are appointed by the founder.

In subsequent convocations, the Board of Directors appoints new members no later than thirty days before the expiry of the mandate.

A member whose mandate has expired continues to perform their duty until the appointment of a new member, and for no longer than ninety days from the expiry of the mandate.

If a new member is not appointed within the period referred to in the preceding paragraph, the decision on appointment is made by the founder. If there is no founder or the founder does not make a decision within a reasonable period, the appointment is decided by the competent authority in accordance with the law.

### Article 15.

Membership of the Board of Directors ceases upon expiry of the mandate, recall, loss of legal capacity, resignation or death.

A member of the Board of Directors is recalled by the Board of Directors by a two-thirds majority of votes, whereby the person whose recall is being voted on has no voting right.

### Article 16.

A member of the Board of Directors and the Director may resign by written notice addressed to the Board of Directors. The resignation takes effect upon the expiry of the tenth day from the day of receipt of the written notice.

### Article 17.

A vacant seat on the Board of Directors is filled in the manner provided for appointment. The mandate of the new member lasts until the expiry of the mandate of their predecessor.

### Article 18.

The Director represents the Foundation, is responsible for the legality of its work, conducts the affairs of the Foundation in accordance with the decisions of the Board of Directors and submits the draft financial plan and annual accounts.

The Director may be a member of the Board of Directors.

### Article 19.

The first Director is appointed by the founder in the founding act.

The Director is appointed and dismissed by the Board of Directors. The mandate of the Director lasts four years, with the possibility of re-election.

Before adopting a decision on dismissal, the Board of Directors is obliged to enable the Director to state their position on the reasons for dismissal.

The Board of Directors dismisses the Director in particular if they do not act conscientiously, use the Foundation's assets in their personal interest, cause damage to the Foundation, or for other justified reasons determined by the Board of Directors.

## V — FOUNDER

### Article 20.

The founder of the Foundation has the rights determined by the law and this Statute.

The founder appoints the first convocation of the Board of Directors.

## VI — TRANSPARENCY OF WORK

### Article 21.

The work of the Foundation is public. Transparency is ensured by publishing the annual activity report, the annual financial report and the decisions of the Board of Directors of importance for users and the public, including decisions on the adoption and amendment of rulebooks, on the programmes referred to in Article 7 of this Statute, and decisions that materially affect the rights of users.

Publication is carried out via the Foundation's website or in another suitable manner.

Every person has the right to inspect the Foundation's documents, in accordance with the law and the Foundation's acts, with respect for the protection of personal data and business secrets.

## VII — GENERAL ACTS

### Article 22.

The general acts of the Foundation are rulebooks, rules of procedure of its bodies, employment rules and other acts governing matters of importance for the Foundation's work.

The general acts of the Foundation are adopted and amended by the Board of Directors by a majority of votes of the total number of members, on the proposal of the Director or a member of the Board of Directors.

The general acts of the Foundation must be in accordance with this Statute.

General acts enter into force on the day determined by them, and at the earliest on the day of publication on the Foundation's website.

## VIII — AMENDMENTS TO THE STATUTE

### Article 23.

The initiative for amending and supplementing the Statute may be submitted by the founder, the Board of Directors or the Director.

The decision on amending and supplementing the Statute is adopted by the Board of Directors by a two-thirds majority of votes of the total number of members.

Amendments and supplements to the Statute enter into force on the day of entry in the Register of Endowments and Foundations.

## IX — STATUS CHANGES AND TERMINATION

### Article 24.

The Board of Directors decides on merger by absorption, merger by formation, division, change of legal form and termination of the Foundation by a two-thirds majority of votes of the total number of members.

### Article 25.

In the event of termination, the remaining assets of the Foundation are allocated to another endowment, foundation or association established for the achievement of the same or similar public-benefit objectives, with preference for those acting in accordance with the principles of the social and solidarity economy.

The decision on allocation is adopted by the Board of Directors within thirty days of the decision on termination. If the Board of Directors does not adopt a decision within that period, the decision on the allocation of the remaining assets is made by the competent authority of the local self-government unit on whose territory the registered office of the Foundation is located.

## X — FINAL PROVISIONS

### Article 26.

The Board of Directors adopts the rulebooks provided for by this Statute within sixty days of its entry into force.

### Article 27.

Matters not regulated by this Statute are governed by the Law on Endowments and Foundations.

### Article 28.

This Statute enters into force on the day of entry in the Register of Endowments and Foundations.

In Sombor, on 16.05.2026.
